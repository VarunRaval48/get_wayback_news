Carole Stimpfle, Gregg Savarese

By VINCENT M. MALLOZZI
Published: January 22, 2010
  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints

Carole Anne Stimpfle, a daughter of Eileen Stimpfle and John B. Stimpfle of Northvale, N.J., was married Saturday evening to Gregg Adam Savarese, the son of Louis A. Savarese, of Randolph, N.J., and the late Ilene W. Savarese. Msgr. Frank Del Prete performed the ceremony at SS. Peter and Paul Church in Hoboken, N.J., with Rabbi Ari Fridkis taking part.

Edgar Galindez

The bride, 36, is taking her husband’s name. She is a clinical social worker at the Jewish Guild for the Blind in New York. She also manages a private clinical therapy practice in New York that specializes in the elderly. She graduated from Bloomsburg University and received a master’s degree in social work from Fordham. Her father retired as a lithographer with Sandy Alexander, a press operator in Clifton, N.J.

The bridegroom, 34, is the chief operating officer of Meetings By Mail, a company in Randolph that digitally records medical meetings and makes them available to doctors for home-study courses. His father is the chief executive of the company.

The bridegroom also is general counsel to HMHTTC Response Incorporated, a national emergency response company in Mount Arlington, N.J. He graduated magna cum laude from the College of New Jersey, and received a law degree from Boston College.

The couple met in June 2005 while playing volleyball in Hoboken. They were on the same recreational team, and Mr. Savarese was thrilled because he had already noticed Ms. Stimpfle.

“She was gorgeous,” he said. “I was very attracted to her.”

Minutes into their first match, the ball sailed over the net and was headed directly between them. Mr. Savarese dived and hit the ball, only to have it carom off Ms. Stimpfle’s face.

She went to the floor.

“I wasn’t too hurt,” she recalled. “I was just kind of stunned.”

So was Mr. Savarese. It was not the way he had intended to make an impression on Ms. Stimpfle.

“I was so embarrassed and kept apologizing profusely,” he said. “Fortunately, she was very good-natured about it.”

They became friends, and in time, Ms. Stimpfle came to know Mr. Savarese as “a wonderful person, someone who is very positive, good-humored and energetic, someone who brings out the real, true self in me, and we bring out the best in one another.”

They still play volleyball together in Hoboken.

“Before every game,” Mr. Savarese said, “I promise not to hit her in the face.”

VINCENT M. MALLOZZI
