Cleveland Musicians Make Case to Audience

  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints
By DANIEL J. WAKIN; Compiled by DAVE ITZKOFF
Published: January 15, 2010

Members of the Cleveland Orchestra were onstage on Thursday night for performances of Strauss’s “Don Juan,” a violin concerto by Thomas Adès and Brahms’s Second Symphony. It was their first night back with their music director, Franz Welser-Möst, since Jan. 6, when they had said that a strike could take place as early as this Monday. About 15 minutes before the concert’s 8 p.m. start time, they left the stage, and a colleague announced that they would be passing through the aisles with leaflets, which is exactly what they did. (Above, the keyboardist Joela Jones.) The leaflets made the performers’ case in a contract dispute with management, which wants them to take a salary cut this season, as staff members and executives have done. The musicians are calling for a freeze in pay at last year’s levels. After the leaflets were distributed, the players made their way back to their chairs, tuned up and started “Don Juan.”
