Critiquing a Critique (1 Letter)

  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints
Published: January 18, 2010

To the Editor:

Skip to next paragraph

RSS Feed

  • Get Science News From The New York Times »

Dr. Richard A. Friedman’s column “Before You Quit Antidepressants” (Mind, Jan. 12) dismissed our finding, published in The Journal of the American Medical Association, that there was little if any benefit of antidepressant drugs, relative to placebo, in depressed patients whose symptoms are mild or moderate in severity.

Dr. Friedman’s critique is misleading. His complaint that only two drugs were included in our analysis overlooks the fact that the many approved antidepressants vary little in effectiveness. His endorsement of studies that screen out placebo responders misses the point that our study aimed to estimate the difference between the effects of medications and placebos.

Finally, his failure to acknowledge findings consistent with our own from two previous reviews is surprising given that both of them used the F.D.A. trials endorsed by Dr. Friedman. Robert J. DeRubeis

Jay C. Fournier

Philadelphia

The writers are researchers at the University of Pennsylvania.
