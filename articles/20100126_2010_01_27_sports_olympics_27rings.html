Lindsey Vonn and Bode Miller Named to U.S. Alpine Team

Streeter Lecka/Getty Images

Hannah Kearney was named to the U.S. women's moguls team with Shannon Bahrke, Heather McPhie and Michelle Roark.

  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints
By BILL PENNINGTON
Published: January 26, 2010

The United States ski team Tuesday announced the 22 Alpine racers who would compete in the 2010 Vancouver Olympics, and although there were no surprises, it did affirm that Bode Miller, barring injury in the next few weeks, would return for his fourth Games.

Miller, who will compete in multiple events, was one of 12 men selected to the team. Lindsey Vonn, who will compete in five events, led the women’s team. Two defending Olympic champions were also in the group: Ted Ligety, who won a gold medal at the 2006 Turin Olympics in the combined, and Julia Mancuso, who won the giant slalom in 2006.

There are some good story lines on the team, including that of Sarah Schleper, who becomes a four-time Olympian nearly two years after the birth of her son, Lasse. The men’s team has a wealth of young talent, including the 23-year-old Andrew Weibrecht, who despite his relative inexperience on the World Cup circuit has had impressive results in several downhill races this season. Joining Weibrecht will be two 20-year-olds: Nolan Kasper and Tommy Ford.

Not selected was the two-time Olympian Scott Macartney, who has been a popular mainstay on the team. Macartney is coming off two years of serious injuries. In 2008, he had a traumatic head injury after a crash in Kitzbühel, Austria. In 2009, a knee ligament tear sidelined him. Macartney made a comeback this season, but his best result has been a 23rd in downhill.

Raised in the state of Washington, he had been looking forward to competing close to home in Vancouver.

Jim Tracy, the United States head women’s coach, praised all his racers, especially Vonn and Schleper.

“With Lindsey, there’s potential in every event,” Tracy said. “She’s put in the hard work to make it happen and has proven she can, but the cream of the crop will be there and they all have the same goal.

“Sarah has achieved a huge goal that a year ago might have been unrealistic. But she has a spirit like no other. If you look at the Olympics, there’s always someone who comes out of nowhere.”

The men’s coach, Sasha Rearick, who had some difficult decisions to make, seemed pleased with the combination of veterans and younger skiers.

“It’s an impressive mix of veterans like Ted and Bode, who’ve shown they can perform when it counts,” Rearick said. “But then we’ve got young guys like Andrew Weibrecht and Tommy Ford who have consistently made progress all winter.”

Rounding out the men’s squad are Steven Nyman and Jimmy Cochran, who competed in Turin; Marco Sullivan, named to two previous Olympic teams; and Will Brandenburg, Erik Fisher, Tim Jitloff and Jake Zamansky.

The women’s squad includes Stacey Cook, who competed in the 2006 Games; and Hailey Duke, Chelsea Marshall, Megan McJames, Alice McKennis, Kaylin Richardson and Leanne Smith.

FREESTYLE TEAM NAMED The World Cup moguls champion Hannah Kearney and the four-time Olympian Daron Rahlves highlight the United States freestyle team for Vancouver.

Kearney will be joined in women’s moguls by the 2002 silver medalist Shannon Bahrke, Michelle Roark and Heather McPhie. Patrick Deneen headlines the men’s moguls team, along with Bryon Wilson, Nate Roberts and Michael Morse.

The two-time Olympian Jeret Peterson leads the men’s aerials and will be joined by the world champion Ryan St. Onge, Matt DePeters and Dylan Ferguson. Lacy Schnoor, Emily Cook, Jana Lindsey and Ashley Caldwell, 16, made the women’s aerials team.

Rahlves and Casey Puckett, former Alpine skiers, will compete in ski cross when the event makes its debut. (AP) SNOWBOARDER FRIEDEN RETIRES Tanja Frieden of Switzerland, who had planned to defend her snowboard cross title in Vancouver, retired from competition after tearing her Achilles’ tendons at a World Cup meet last week in Stoneham, Canada. (AP)

SPEEDSKATER DENIED The five-time Olympic speedskating champion Claudia Pechstein of Germany lost her last chance to have her doping ban lifted in time for Vancouver. The Swiss Federal Tribunal refused to suspend Pechstein’s two-year ban for blood doping. The tribunal still must consider Pechstein’s legal challenge in its entirety. (AP)
