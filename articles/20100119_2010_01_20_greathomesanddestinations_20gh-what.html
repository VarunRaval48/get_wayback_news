WHAT: A two-bedroom two-bath condo in downtown Memphis

HOW MUCH: $355,000

SIZE: 2,430 square feet

PER SQUARE FOOT: $146.09

SETTING: This apartment is one of 122 in the South Bluff Lofts, a 1909 building originally used as a distribution center for a wholesale supply company. The surrounding neighborhood is known as the South Main Arts District, a section of downtown that has a farmer’s market, galleries and boutiques. The Arcade Restaurant, family-owned since 1919, is nearby, as is Ernestine and Hazel’s, a bar and grill said to have a past as a brothel. Beale Street and Memphis’s central business district are within walking distance. A trolley that runs through downtown stops in front of the building.

INSIDE: The apartment has a triangular layout. A long hallway leads to the living-and-dining area. The master bedroom has a walk-in closet. The guest bedroom has southwestern exposure and two adjoining walls of windows overlooking the Mississippi River. Ceilings throughout are over 14 feet high; floors are of sealed concrete. Most of the building’s brick walls and ductwork were left exposed.

OUTDOOR SPACE: There is a shared roof deck with a sauna, a lap pool and an exercise facility.

TAXES: $6,602 a year; homeowner association dues: $636.63 a month (covers cable, wi-fi and general maintenance)

CONTACT: Ashley Dacus, Henry Turley Realtors (901) 485-2179; henryturleyrealtors.com

CERRILLOS, N.M.

WHAT: A two-bedroom one-bath adobe

HOW MUCH: $350,000

SIZE: 1,000 square feet

PER SQUARE FOOT: $350

SETTING: Cerrillos, population 250, is 15 miles south of Santa Fe. In the late 1800s, it supported as many as 3,000 prospectors mining the Cerrillos Hills for minerals. A bar and a church are both nearby, but for schools, grocery stores, restaurants and shopping, drive to Santa Fe.

INSIDE: According to the listing agent, the house was built in the late 19th century for miners, and renovated within the past 10 years by an architect and designer. There are several original details, including vigas (support beams) and wood floors. There is a commercial range in the kitchen. The main living space, including the living, dining and kitchen areas, has a corrugated tin ceiling. There is a soaking tub in the bathroom. The second bedroom is currently used as a sitting area. What was once an outbuilding is now used as a studio.

OUTDOOR SPACE: There is a brick patio; the yard is fenced.

TAXES: $1,608 a year

CONTACT: Jeff Harakal, Sotheby’s International Realty, the Santa Fe Team (505) 231-2010; santafeteam.com

GLOUCESTER, MASS.

WHAT: A three-bedroom one-bath house

HOW MUCH: $350,000

SIZE: 1,726 square feet

PER SQUARE FOOT: $202.78

SETTING: This house, set into an embankment, is about 10 minutes outside Gloucester, a city of about 30,000 some 25 miles north of Boston. The house is a mile from Wingaersheek Beach, one of several public beaches along Ipswich and Sandy Bay. Shopping, dining and schools can be found in and around the downtown.

INSIDE: The first floor has a living room with a fireplace, and an open kitchen-dining area. Cabinets in the kitchen have etched glass. There are hand-painted glass panels on doors throughout the house. Up a spiral staircase, a second-floor den opens to the backyard. The house’s three bedrooms are also upstairs. The master has a walk-in closet, built-in storage and an adjoining sitting room. There is a skylight in the master bath. The other two bedrooms, both with lofted sleeping areas are on the other side of the house. An outbuilding — used as an art studio by the current owner — has a wood-burning stove.

OUTDOOR SPACE: There is a yard off the second-level den.

TAXES: $3,311 a year

CONTACT: Amanda Armstrong, By the Sea Sotheby’s International Realty (978) 879-6322; realtybytheseaus.com
