Theater Listings: Jan. 8 14

  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
By THE NEW YORK TIMES
Published: January 7, 2010

Approximate running times are in parentheses. Theaters are in Manhattan unless otherwise noted. Full reviews of current shows, additional listings, showtimes and tickets: nytimes.com/theater.

“Brief Encounter”: Tristan Sturrock and Hannah Yelland star in this adaptation of the 1945 film, an import from the British-based Kneehigh Theater Company, directed by Emma Rice. It continues through Jan. 17 at St. Ann’s Warehouse in Brooklyn.

Previews and Openings

‘AGES OF THE MOON’ Previews start on Jan 12. Opens on Jan. 27. Stephen Rea and Sean McGinley bicker and battle in the Abbey Theater production of Sam Shepard’s new drama about a clash between old friends. Atlantic Theater, 336 West 20th Street, Chelsea , (212) 279-4200, ticketcentral.com.20100107

‘A VIEW FROM THE BRIDGE’ In previews; opens on Jan. 24. Scarlett Johansson and Liev Schreiber should make this Arthur Miller revival a hot ticket. Cort Theater, 138 West 48th Street , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com.20100107

‘VENUS IN FUR’ Previews start on Wednesday. Opens on Jan. 26. Walter Bobbie directs this new David Ives play, which goes backstage at an audition. Classic Stage Company, 136 East 13th Street, East Village , (212) 352-3101, classicstage.org.20100107

Broadway

‘BURN THE FLOOR’ A nonstop exhibition of pop ballroom dancing, directed and choreographed by Jason Gilkison. A little tacky and vulgar, but would you want your steamy cha-chas and tangos any other way? (2:05). Longacre Theater, 220 West 48th Street , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Charles Isherwood)20100107

‘BYE BYE BIRDIE’ Robert Longbottom’s color-coded revival of this 1960 musical satire is a painful example of misapplied talent. The unlucky cast members include John Stamos, Gina Gershon and Bill Irwin (2:20). Henry Miller’s Theater, 124 West 43rd Street , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Ben Brantley)20100107

★ ‘FELA!’ This transporting musical biography of a Nigerian revolutionary of song doesn’t so much tell a story as soak an audience to, and through, the skin with the style and sensibility called Afrobeat. There has never been anything on Broadway like Bill T. Jones’s kinetic production, which stars the charismatic Sahr Ngaujah and Kevin Mambo (2:30). Eugene O’Neill Theater, 230 West 49th Street , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Brantley)20100107

★ ‘FINIAN’S RAINBOW’ A glorious cast restores the luster of this oddball 1947 musical with a first-rate score by E. Y. Harburg and Burton Lane. Kate Baldwin and Cheyenne Jackson are the frisky lovers, Jim Norton the wily Irishman with a purloined pot of gold, and Christopher Fitzgerald the leprechaun on the lam. Daft? Yes, but heaven for musical theater lovers (2:25) St. James Theater, 246 West 44th Street , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Isherwood)20100107

‘GOD OF CARNAGE’ Christine Lahti, Annie Potts, Jimmy Smits and Ken Stott are the able new combatants in Yasmina Reza’s shallow but entertaining comedy about the beastly ways of husbands and wives (1:30). Bernard Jacobs Theater, 242 West 45th Street , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Isherwood)20100107

★ ‘HAIR’ (Tony Award for best musical revival.) Diane Paulus’s thrilling, emotionally rich revival of the 1967 rock musical delivers what Broadway otherwise hasn’t felt this season: the intense, unadulterated joy and anguish of that bipolar state called youth. A production that finds the complexity and deep ambivalence in a show people tend to remember as a feel-good free-for-all (2:20). Al Hirschfeld Theater, 302 West 45th Street , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Brantley)20100107

‘A LITTLE NIGHT MUSIC’ Trevor Nunn’s nudgingly acted, lugubriously slow revival of Stephen Sondheim and Hugh Wheeler’s erotic waltz of a musical is most notable for the participation of the lustrous Catherine Zeta-Jones, in a lively (if miscast) Broadway debut, and of the indomitable (and invaluable) Angela Lansbury (2:50). Walter Kerr Theater, 219 West 48th Street , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Brantley)20100107

‘MEMPHIS’ In the city of the title in the 1950s, a pioneering white radio D.J. (the endearing Chad Kimball) falls in love with R&B music and one of its comely practitioners (the lovely Montego Glover) in this slick but essentially soulless musical written by David Bryan and Joe DiPietro (“The Toxic Avenger”) and fleetly directed by Christopher Ashley. Shubert Theater, 225 West 44th Street , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Isherwood)20100107

★ ‘NEXT TO NORMAL’ No show on Broadway makes as direct a grab for the heart — or wrings it as thoroughly — as this brave, breathtaking musical. Tom Kitt and Brian Yorkey’s portrait of a manic-depressive suburban mom (the astounding Alice Ripley) and her family, directed by Michael Greif, isn’t a feel-good musical. It’s a feel-everything musical (2:20). Booth Theater, 222 West 45th Street , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Brantley)20100107

‘RACE’ David Mamet’s surprisingly slack examination of a hot-button topic registers well within the average Broadway audience’s comfort zone. There are glimmers of the author’s scalpel-edge intelligence, and James Spader is excellent as a white lawyer representing a white man accused of raping a black woman. David Alan Grier, Richard Thomas and Kerry Washington round out the able cast (1:40). Ethel Barrymore Theater, 243 West 47th Street , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Brantley)20100107

★ ‘ROCK OF AGES’ The Sunset Strip in the 1980s is the setting for this absurdly enjoyable jukebox musical weaving more than two dozen arena-rock hits from the glory years of MTV — from Journey and Pat Benatar to Poison and Whitesnake — into a story of heartbreak and warped-vinyl dreams. Big hair, songs that are trashy fun, fabulous cast (2:25). Brooks Atkinson Theater, 256 West 47th Street , (212) 307-4100, ticketmaster.com. (Isherwood)20100107

★ ‘WEST SIDE STORY’ In a startlingly sweet revival of this epochal musical, Arthur Laurents trades insolence for innocence, with dividends and losses. If the Jets and the Sharks seem too cute to be true, the show’s doomed lovers (played by Josefina Scaglione and Matt Cavenaugh) have rarely been more affecting. And as Anita, Karen Olivo is a stunner (2:35). Palace Theater, 1564 Broadway, near 46th Street , (212) 307-4100, ticketmaster.com. (Brantley)20100107

★ ‘WISHFUL DRINKING’ Carrie Fisher’s brut-dry, deeply funny one-woman show allows you to see the world through the self-dissociating gaze of a child of Hollywood who has always lived in a fishbowl (2:20). Studio 54, 254 West 54th Street , (212) 719-1300, roundabouttheatre.org. (Brantley)20100107

Off Broadway

★ ‘AVENUE Q’ Even without legs of their own, the singing hand puppets of this Tony-winning musical are still standing tall in their new Off Broadway digs. This “Sesame Street”-style show about being young and jobless in New York remains fresh, in all senses of the word (2:15) New World Stages, 340 West 50th Street, Clinton , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Brantley)20100107

★ ‘BRIEF ENCOUNTER’ This captivating adaptation of the 1945 film, a British import from the Kneehigh Theater Company, may traffic in the antics of classic stage spoofery, but its real raison d’être is to love, honor and obey the spirit of the movie that inspired it. Emma Rice is the inventive director (1:35). St. Ann’s Warehouse, 38 Water Street, at Dock Street, Dumbo, Brooklyn , (718) 254-8779, stannswarehouse.org. (Brantley)20100107

‘CIRCLE MIRROR TRANSFORMATION’ Annie Baker’s absorbing, unblinking and sharply funny one-act takes place in a “creative drama” class in Vermont, where relationships form and fold, secrets are revealed, and everyone gets in touch with his or her inner child (1:45). Playwrights Horizons Peter J. Sharp Theater, 416 West 42nd Street, Clinton , (212) 279-4200, ticketcentral.com. (Anita Gates)20100107

‘CIRCUMCISE ME’ Yisrael Campbell’s highly entertaining account of his conversion to Judaism — not once but three times — is elevated beyond shtick by the recognition that, aside from the jokes, this is Mr. Campbell’s life, and that his quest for a “relationship with God” continues even after the house lights rise (1:30). Bleecker Street Theater, 45 Bleecker Street, East Village , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Daniel M. Gold)20100107

★ ‘THE EMPEROR JONES’ Ciaran O’Reilly’s inspired, poetic production of Eugene O’Neill’s short, brutal drama — starring a wondrous John Douglas Thompson — finds Jungian echoes of universal resonance in a work often perceived as a dated portrait of the black man’s burden (1:10). Soho Playhouse, 15 Vandam Street , (212) 691-1555, sohoplayhouse.com. (Brantley)20100107

‘LOVE, LOSS, AND WHAT I WORE’ Or, shoes, bags and heartaches. A stuffed closet of comic or poignant stories, or both, collected by Nora and Delia Ephron, about women and their relationship to clothes. Featuring a rotating cast of five comics and actors. Westside Downstairs, 407 West 43rd Street, Clinton , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Isherwood)20100107

★ ‘MISALLIANCE’; A delightful revival of one of Shaw’s breeziest comedies. If it is one of his talkier plays, it is also very witty, and this Pearl production gives full voice to Shaw’s litany of sins committed in the name of English propriety (2:30). City Center, 131 West 55th Street, Manhattan , (212) 581-1212, nycitycenter.org. (Wilborn Hampton)20100107

‘NEWSICAL THE MUSICAL’ The four performers in this topical revue — Christina Bianco, Rory O’Malley, Christine Pedi and Michael West — are skilled mimics, but the format sure seems tired, especially when the show delves into politics, where jokes have a shelf life measured in hours. Even the Tiger Woods humor, relatively freshly installed, seems wearying. But some of the less time-bound material works nicely, especially Ms. Bianco’s Celine Dion impression (1:30). 47th Street Theater, 304 West 47th Street, Clinton , (212) 279-4200, ticketcentral.com. (Neil Genzlinger)20100107

★ ‘THE ORPHANS’ HOME CYCLE’: PART ONE: ‘THE STORY OF A CHILDHOOD’ On the basis of the three-work production that begins this New York premiere, Horton Foote’s heart-piercing, nine-play family album about growing up lonely in early-20th-century Texas should be the great adventure of the theater season. Directed with cinematic fluidity and novelistic detail by Michael Wilson (2:50). Signature Theater at Peter Norton Space, 555 West 42nd Street, Clinton , (212) 244-7529, signaturetheatre.org. (Brantley)20100107

★ ‘OUR TOWN’ David Cromer strips away the folksy warmth in which Thornton Wilder’s play is often saturated to reveal this classic’s quietly mournful depths. This wonderfully intimate production puts a small audience smack in the middle of Grover’s Corners (2:00). Barrow Street Theater, 27 Barrow Street, West Village , (212) 868-4444, smarttix.com. (Isherwood)20100107

‘THE UNDERSTUDY’ Julie White fabricates bountiful laughs from a sumptuous display of strained nerves in Theresa Rebeck’s scattershot backstage comedy, which also stars Justin Kirk (“Weeds”) and Mark-Paul Gosselaar (“Saved by the Bell”) as actors on far different levels of the showbiz totem pole (1:30). Laura Pels Theater, 111 West 46th Street , (212) 719-1300, roundabouttheatre.org. (Isherwood)20100107

‘ZERO HOUR’ Jim Brochu does an excellent imitation of Zero Mostel in his standard-issue bio-play (1:50). Theater at Saint Clement’s, 423 West 46th Street, Clinton , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Jason Zinoman)20100107

Off Off Broadway

‘RED NOIR’ An elaborate, abstract and scattershot play by the poet Anne Waldman, directed by the inveterate avant-gardist Judith Malina, “Red Noir” is participatory theater of the old Lower East Side school. It’s confusing and exhausting but sometimes exhilarating, and greatly aided by its largely youthful and idealistic cast (1:25). the Living Theater, 21 Clinton Street, Lower East Side , (212) 352-3101, theatermania.com. (Webster)20100107

★ ‘ROMEO AND JULIET’ The Nature Theater of Oklahoma’s inspired riff on Shakespeare’s classic consists of verbatim monologues in which people recount all they know about the star-crossed lovers. These variously informed, confused, chaotic and plain crazy soliloquies are performed with fake-histrionic brilliance by Anne Gridley and Robert M. Johanson (1:30). the Kitchen, 512 West 19th Street, Chelsea , (212) 255-5793, thekitchen.org. (Isherwood)20100107

Extravaganzas

★ ‘BIG APPLE CIRCUS: BELLO IS BACK!’ The high-haired clown Bello Nock returns to the big top, along with an astonishingly talented group of exciting performers (1:50). Damrosch Park at Lincoln Center , (888) 541-3750, bigapplecircus.org. (Ken Jaworowski)20100107

Long-Running Shows

‘ALFRED HITCHCOCK’S THE 39 STEPS’ A theatrical riff on the 1935 Hitchcock movie (1:45). Helen Hayes Theater, 240 West 44th Street , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Brantley)20100107

‘BILLY ELLIOT THE MUSICAL’ Ballet dreams in coal country(2:50). Imperial Theater, 249 West 45th Street , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Brantley)20100107

‘CELEBRITY AUTOBIOGRAPHY: IN THEIR OWN WORDS’ A rotating cast of comics and actors reads selections of the witless wisdom culled from the tell-all tomes of the rich and famous (1:30). Triad Theater, 158 West 72nd Street , (212) 868-4444, smarttix.com. (Isherwood)20100107

‘CHICAGO’ Jazz Age sex, murder and razzle-dazzle (2:25). Ambassador Theater, 219 West 49th Street , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Brantley)20100107

‘THE FANTASTICKS’ Boy meets girl, forever (2:05). Snapple Theater Center, 210 West 50th Street , (212) 307-4100, ticketmaster.com. (Brantley)20100107

‘FUERZA BRUTA: LOOK UP’ A sensory bath aimed at clubgoing college kids in search of cultural diversion (1:05). Daryl Roth Theater, 20 Union Square East, at 15th Street , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Isherwood)20100107

‘GAUGUIN/SAVAGE LIGHT’ George Fischoff’s one-man musical about Paul Gauguin. Nola Studios, 250 West 54th Street, Clinton , (212) 868-4444, smarttix.com. (Genzlinger)20100107

‘IN THE HEIGHTS’ A musical panorama of a neighborhood in Washington Heights undergoing change (2:20). Richard Rodgers Theater, 226 West 46th Street , (212) 307-4100, ticketmaster.com. (Isherwood)20100107

‘JERSEY BOYS’ The biomusical that walks like a man (2:30). August Wilson Theater, 245 West 52nd Street , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Brantley)20100107

‘THE LION KING’ Disney’s call of the wild (2:45). Minskoff Theater, 200 West 45th Street, at Broadway , (212) 307-4100, ticketmaster.com. (Brantley)20100107

‘MAMMA MIA!’ The jukebox musical set to the disco throb of Abba (2:20). Cadillac Winter Garden Theater, 1634 Broadway, at 50th Street , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Brantley)20100107

‘MARY POPPINS’ Supernanny (2:30). New Amsterdam Theater, 214 West 42nd Street , (212) 307-4747, ticketmaster.com. (Brantley)20100107

‘MY FIRST TIME’ Ken Davenport’s venture into Web-theater synergy mines a site called MyFirstTime.com to cull stories of inaugural encounters with sex (1:30). New World Stages, 340 West 50th Street, Clinton , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Ginia Bellafante)20100107

‘THE PHANTOM OF THE OPERA’ Who was that masked man, anyway? (2:30). Majestic Theater, 247 West 44th Street , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Brantley)20100107

‘SOUTH PACIFIC’ A 1949 tale of men and women unmoored by war (2:50). Vivian Beaumont Theater, 150 West 65th Street, Lincoln Center , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Brantley)20100107

‘WICKED’ Oz revisited (2:45). Gershwin Theater, 222 West 51st Street , (212) 307-4100, ticketmaster.com. (Brantley)20100107

Last Chance

‘ALTAR BOYZ’; closes on Sunday. A satirical show about a Christian pop group made up of five potential Teen People cover boys (1:30). New World Stages, 340 West 50th Street, Clinton , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Isherwood)20100107

‘DO NOT GO GENTLE’; closes on Sunday. This one-man show about the poet Dylan Thomas forgoes plot to focus on the writer’s beautiful words (1:35). Clurman Theater, 410 West 42nd Street, Clinton , (212) 279-4200, ticketcentral.com. (Jaworowski)20100107

★ ‘IN THE NEXT ROOM, OR THE VIBRATOR PLAY’; closes on Sunday. Sarah Ruhl’s delightful new play is set in the late 19th century in the adjoining parlor and consulting room of a doctor who specializes in treating “hysteria” in women with the use of a newfangled electrified aid. Michael Cerveris is the kindly doctor, Laura Benanti his curious wife. A real novelty: a sex comedy written for adults (2:30). Lyceum Theater, 149 West 45th Street , (212) 239-6200, telecharge.com. (Isherwood)20100107

‘RAGTIME’; closes on Sunday. Marcia Milgrom Dodge’s appealingly modest interpretation of this 1998 musical about early-20th-century American growing pains, adapted from E. L. Doctorow’s novel, finds an affecting, uneasy human soul within the work’s panoramic sweep. This production doesn’t disguise its hearty preachiness, but it elicits glimmers of delicate beauty (2:45). Neil Simon Theater, 250 West 52nd Street , (212) 307-4100, ticketmaster.com. (Brantley)20100107

Correction: January 9, 2010
A theater entry in the Listings pages on Friday about “Misalliance,” at City Center, misstated the play’s closing date and was erroneously included among the “Last Chance” entries. The play closes on Jan. 24, not Sunday.

An article in the Escapes pages on Jan. 1 about visiting Boston in the winter, which described modern and historical sites, misstated the year of Paul Revere’s famous ride. It was 1775, not 1776. The article also referred incorrectly to the age of his house. While some rooms are decorated in an 18th-century manner and Revere bought the house in 1770, it was built in the 17th century. It is not an 18th-century house.
