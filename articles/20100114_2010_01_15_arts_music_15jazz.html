Published: January 14, 2010

JOHN ABERCROMBIE ORGAN QUARTET (Friday through Sunday) No stranger to Hammond B-3 organ combos, the guitarist John Abercrombie joins forces here with the organist Gary Versace and the drummer Adam Nussbaum, as well as a special guest: the alto saxophonist Greg Osby. At 8:30 and 10:30 p.m., Iridium, 1650 Broadway, at 51st Street , (212) 582-2121, iridiumjazzclub.com; cover, $30, with a $10 minimum. (Nate Chinen)20100114

AMPERSAND (Wednesday) The guitarist Tim Ziesmer features his own rock-influenced compositions in this mutable band, featuring Sam Sadigursky on tenor saxophone, Nate Radley on bass and Peter Retzlaff on drums. At 9 and 10:30 p.m., Tea Lounge, 837 Union Street, near Sixth Avenue, Park Slope, Brooklyn , (718) 789-2762, tealoungeny.com; $5 suggested donation. (Chinen)20100114

JOANNE BRACKEEN TRIO (Friday and Saturday) The pianist JoAnne Brackeen favors a whimsical but sophisticated sort of post-bop; her trio features Ugonna Okegwo on bass and Adam Cruz on drums. At 8 and 10 p.m., Kitano Hotel, 66 Park Avenue, at 38th Street , (212) 885-7119, kitano.com; cover, $25, with a $15 minimum. (Chinen)20100114

GEORGE COLEMAN QUARTET (Friday through Sunday) George Coleman, a tenor saxophonist with a brusque but soulful style, advances his vision of hard bop with a band that includes Harold Mabern on piano, Nat Reeves on bass and George Coleman Jr. on drums. At 7:30 and 9:30 p.m., Jazz Standard, 116 East 27th Street, Manhattan , (212) 576-2232, jazzstandard.net; $30. (Chinen)20100114

ROY CAMPBELL’S AKHENATEN/MATANA ROBERTS’S ILLUMINATION (Thursday) Drawing on the expressive and searching music from his most recent album, “Akhenaten Suite” (2008), the trumpeter Roy Campbell leads a group with Bryan Carrott on vibraphone, Hilliard Greene on bass and Michael Wemberly on drums. Ms. Roberts, an alto saxophonist, pursues her own visions with a band that includes the cornetist Graham Haynes, the pianist Gabriel Guerrero, the harpist Shelley Burgon and the drummer Damion Reid. At 8 p.m., Roulette, 20 Greene Street, at Grand Street, SoHo , (212) 219-8242, roulette.org; $15; $10 for members, students and those under 30. (Chinen)20100114

JIMMY COBB’S REMINISCENCE BAND (Thursday) Jimmy Cobb, the venerable hard-bop drummer, has done his fair share of reminiscing lately — last year he was the go-to guy for all manner of “Kind of Blue” commemoration — but he hasn’t lost himself to nostalgia, at least not entirely. He’ll play Miles Davis music here, but with a savvy younger crew: the trumpeter Randy Brecker, the saxophonist Javon Jackson, the pianist George Cables and the bassist Eddie Gomez. (Through Jan. 24.) At 8:30 and 10:30 p.m., Iridium, 1650 Broadway, at 51st Street , (212) 582-2121, iridiumjazzclub.com; cover, $30, with a $10 minimum. (Chinen)20100114

★ CELEBRATING CHICK COREA (Saturday) Mr. Corea, the prolific and ever-versatile keyboardist and composer, receives a knowing salute organized by the saxophonist Tim Garland, one of his former sidemen. Among the assembled talent: the vibraphonist Gary Burton, with whom Mr. Corea has recorded some celebrated duet albums; the pianist Geoffrey Keezer, one of his many respectful heirs; the trombonist Steve Davis, another alumnus of his employ; and the Harlem String Quartet. At 8 p.m., Symphony Space, 2537 Broadway, at 95th Street , (212) 864-5400, symphonyspace.org; $35; day of show $40; members $30. (Chinen)20100114

EVOLVING MUSIC/EVOLVING VOICE (Monday) This weekly series features improvised music from the experimental side of the tracks, in a diverse array. At 7 p.m., things kick off with a duo of the vocalist Katie Bull and the bassist Joe Fonda; at 8, Yoon Sun Choi sings with backing from Jacob Sacks on keyboards and Vinnie Sperrazza on drums. The 9 p.m. slot will feature the saxophonist and trumpeter Joe McPhee, along with the bassist Dominic Duval and the trumpeter Jean-Luc Cappozzo; the final set, at 10, goes to Basso Continuo, a group led by the tenor saxophonist Stephen Gauci, with the trumpeter Herb Robertson and two bassists, Ken Filiano and Mike Bisio. The Local 269, 269 East Houston Street, at Suffolk Street, Lower East Side , (212) 254-5420, rucma.org; $20. (Chinen)20100114

★ FLY (Thursday) The tenor saxophonist Mark Turner, the bassist Larry Grenadier and the drummer Jeff Ballard make up this superb trio, which eschews the usual horn-plus-rhythm hierarchy in the service of an elastic group ideal. The band’s most recent album, “Sky & Country” (ECM), was among the best jazz releases of last year. (Through Jan. 24.) At 7:30 and 9:30 p.m., Jazz Standard, 116 East 27th Street, Manhattan , (212) 576-2232, jazzstandard.net; $25. (Chinen)20100114

★ FORWARD FLIGHT (Friday and Saturday) Subtitled “A Gathering of Brass in Contemporary Music,” this four-day affair is a subseries of the Festival of New Trumpet Music. It wraps up this weekend with a lot of intelligent ruckus, notably through sets by the Chicago Underground Duo, which features the cornetist Rob Mazurek (Friday at 7:30 p.m.), and the Open Circuit International Trumpet Ensemble, a trans-Atlantic assemblage spearheaded by Taylor Ho Bynum, one of the festival’s chief organizers (Saturday at 9). For a full schedule, fontmusic.net. Abrons Arts Center, 466 Grand Street, at Pitt Street, Lower East Side , (212) 598-0400; $15; $12 for students. (Chinen)20100114

TOMAS FUJIWARA AND THE HOOK UP (Friday) Mr. Fujiwara’s alert drumming has propelled some excellent ensembles on the new-music landscape. Here he presents his own open-ended compositions for a band with Jonathan Finlayson on trumpet, Brian Settles on tenor saxophone, Mary Halvorson on guitar and Danton Boller on bass. At 8 p.m., the Stone, Avenue C and Second Street, East Village , thestonenyc.com; cover, $10. (Chinen)20100114

PEDRO GIRAUDO JAZZ ORCHESTRA (Friday and Saturday) Pedro Giraudo, an Argentine bassist and composer, draws here from a worthwhile self-released album, “El Viaje,” with a band of resourceful young improvisers. At 9 and 10:30 p.m., Jazz Gallery, 290 Hudson Street, at Spring Street, South Village , (212) 242-1063, jazzgallery.org; $15; $10 for members. (Chinen)20100114

★ FRED HERSCH TRIO (Friday through Sunday) For much of the last decade, the pianist Fred Hersch led a graceful and purposeful trio with Drew Gress on bass and Nasheet Waits on drums. Here he re-enlists Mr. Gress but joins forces with the drummer Paul Motian, with whom he has played just once before (in Charlie Haden’s Liberation Music Orchestra, for a single week in the 1980s). Mr. Motian is a stubborn poet of shading and pulse, and for someone as conscientious as Mr. Hersch, he should be a terrifically provocative foil. At 9 and 11 p.m., Village Vanguard, 178 Seventh Avenue South, at 11th Street, West Village , (212) 255-4037, villagevanguard.com; cover, $25, with a $10 minimum. (Chinen)20100114

ARI HOENIG QUARTET (Sunday and Monday) Ari Hoenig approaches jazz drumming with manic glee, and yet he’s nothing if not exacting about his craft. His fine working band includes players of similar temperament: the guitarist Gilad Hekselman, the pianist Tigran Hamasyan and the bassist Orlando Le Fleming. Sunday at 9:30 p.m., 55 Bar, 55 Christopher Street, West Village, (212) 929-9883, 55bar.com; cover, $10, with a two-drink minimum. Monday at 9 and 10:30 p.m., Smalls, 183 West 10th Street, West Village , (212) 252-5091, smallsjazzclub.com; cover, $20. (Chinen)20100114

★ THE JAZZ MASTERS QUINTET (Friday through Sunday) Coming on the heels of this year’s National Endowment for the Arts Jazz Masters Awards Ceremony and Concert, this commemorative engagement features two freshman inductees, the vibraphonist Bobby Hutcherson and the pianist Cedar Walton. Rounding out the group are a few future prospects: the multireedist James Spaulding, the bassist David Williams and the drummer Al Foster. At 7:30 and 9:30 p.m., Dizzy’s Club Coca-Cola, Frederick P. Rose Hall, Jazz at Lincoln Center, 60th Street and Broadway , (212) 258-9595, jalc.org; cover, $30 and $35, with a minimum of $10 at tables, $5 at the bar. (Chinen)20100114

JONATHAN KANTOR QUINTET (Monday) Jonathan Kantor is an earnest and ambitious young tenor saxophonist who works by day (and night) as a publicist for the Blue Note. He makes his debut as a leader there with this talent-heavy band, featuring the pianist Kenny Werner, the trumpeter Alex Sipiagin, the bassist Gerald Cannon and the drummer Adam Nussbaum. At 8 and 10:30 p.m., Blue Note, 131 West Third Street, Greenwich Village , (212) 475-8592, bluenote.net; $10 at tables, $5 at the bar. (Chinen)20100114

★ LEE KONITZ TRIO (Tuesday through Thursday) Now in his 80s, the alto saxophonist Lee Konitz is a bona fide jazz elder, though he wears that distinction lightly: always an explorer, he has continued to experiment with style and format, never settling in one place for long. His trio here includes Dan Tepfer, the pianist with whom he recorded a recent duo album, and Matt Wilson, a drummer with whom he has worked on and off for years. (Through Jan. 24.) At 9 and 11 p.m., Village Vanguard, 178 Seventh Avenue South, at 11th Street, West Village , (212) 255-4037, villagevanguard.com; cover, $25, with a $10 minimum. (Chinen)20100114

BEN MONDER TRIO/JOE SANDERS TRIO (Friday) Ben Monder, a guitarist known for his beguilingly fluid technique, performs a pair of early sets, at 7 and 9 p.m., with a working trio. At 11 p.m. and 12:30 a.m. the bassist Joe Sanders, the second runner-up in last year’s Thelonious Monk International Jazz Competition, leads a trio of his own. Bar Next Door, 129 Macdougal Street, Greenwich Village , (212) 529-5945, lalanternacaffe.com; cover, $6 at 7 p.m. and 12:30 a.m.; $12 at 9 and 11 p.m.; with a one-drink minimum. (Chinen)20100114

JOE MORRIS TRIO/FLAHERTY, CORSANO, CARTER (Tuesday) Joe Morris, a guitarist who applies the clear tone of Jim Hall to darker and pricklier purposes, has close history with the pianist Steve Lantner and the drummer Luther Gray, with whom he plays an 11 p.m. set. A preceding set, at 10, will feature quick-flash collective improvisation by the saxophonist Paul Flaherty, the drummer Chris Corsano and the multireedist and trumpeter Daniel Carter. Bowery Poetry Club, 308 Bowery, near Bleecker Street, East Village , bowerypoetry.com; cover, $10. (Chinen)20100114

★ THE MUSIC OF TITO PUENTE AND DIZZY GILLESPIE (Friday and Saturday) Carlos Henriquez, the adaptable young bassist in the Jazz at Lincoln Center Orchestra, steps to the foreground in this tribute to the ageless big-band Latin jazz of Puente and Gillespie. In addition to the orchestra, he’ll have some convincing firepower in the form of the Cuban drummer Ignacio Berroa and the conga master Giovanni Hidalgo. At 8 p.m., Rose Theater, Frederick P. Rose Hall, Jazz at Lincoln Center, 60th Street and Broadway , (212) 721-6500, jalc.org; $10 to $95. (Chinen)20100114

FRANK WESS CELEBRATION NONET (Tuesday through Thursday) At 88, Frank Wess conveys a gentlemanly cool on tenor saxophone and a marvelous expressiveness on flute. Though recognized as a National Endowment for the Arts Jazz Master, he hasn’t coasted, as he proves on a recent album, “Once Is Not Enough” (Labeth), with a version of this band. Among the special guests joining them for this birthday run are the pianists Hank Jones, Mulgrew Miller, Bill Charlap and Renee Rosnes. (Through Jan. 24.) At 7:30 and 9:30 p.m., Dizzy’s Club Coca-Cola, Frederick P. Rose Hall, Jazz at Lincoln Center, 60th Street and Broadway , (212) 258-9595, jalc.org; cover, $30, with a minimum of $10 at tables, $5 at the bar. (Chinen)20100114

JEAN ROHE (Monday) Ms. Rohe is a confident and worldly young singer whose original compositions tend toward a coolly imploring tone. Performing with the accordionist Liam Robinson and the guitarist Ilusha Tsinadze, she draws from her recent debut, “Lead Me Home.” At 8:30 and 10:30 p.m., Bar Next Door, 129 Macdougal Street, Greenwich Village , (212) 529-5945, lalanternacaffe.com; cover, $12, with a one-drink minimum. (Chinen)20100114

ROSWELL RUDD QUARTET (Wednesday) Roswell Rudd is a spirited and wide-open trombonist, just as happy with a free-form rustle as he is with rambunctious swing. He draws here from “Keep Your Heart Right” (Sunnyside), with a quartet that includes Lafayette Harris on piano, Ken Filiano on bass and Sunny Kim on vocals. At 8:30 and 10:30 p.m., Iridium, 1650 Broadway, at 51st Street , (212) 582-2121, iridiumjazzclub.com; cover, $20, with a $10 minimum. (Chinen)20100114

JOE SAMPLE TRIO (Friday through Sunday) The pianist Joe Sample has stayed true to the cause of melody and groove ever since his days with the Crusaders, which he recently regrouped. His most recent album, “The Song Lives On” (Pra), is a typical offering: sumptuous and sweet, but not without sustenance. At 8 and 10:30 p.m., Blue Note, 131 West Third Street, Greenwich Village , (212) 475-8592, bluenote.net; cover, $45 at tables; $30 at the bar; with a $5 minimum. (Chinen)20100114

ARTURO SANDOVAL (Thursday) Mr. Sandoval is a Cuban trumpeter best known for stratospheric bravado: he is capable of subtlety, but not predisposed toward it. His most recent album, “Rumba Palace” (Telarc), celebrates the Latin dance halls of yore with an earnest exuberance that should only sound better in person. (Through Jan. 24.) At 8 and 10:30 p.m., Blue Note, 131 West Third Street, Greenwich Village , (212) 475-8592, bluenote.net; cover, $35 at tables; $20 at the bar; with a $5 minimum. (Chinen)20100114

‘SEEING JAZZ WITH GEORGE WEIN’ (Thursday) Mr. Wein, founder of the Newport Jazz Festival and a number of its offshoots, will serve as host here, showing images from his art and photography collection and reflecting on his life in jazz. The musical component will come from the clarinetist Anat Cohen and the guitarists Howard Alden and Gene Bertoncini, musicians for whom he has special admiration. At 7:30 p.m., Symphony Space, 2537 Broadway, at 95th Street , (212) 864-5400, symphonyspace.org; $25; day of show, $30; $20 for members. (Chinen)20100114

NATE SMITH GROUP (Thursday) As a member of Chris Potter’s Underground, Nate Smith has cornered the market on shrewdly fractured funk, full of odd feints and responsive parries. He seems likely to pursue similar strategies here, in a band with Jaleel Shaw on alto saxophone, Aaron Parks on keyboards, Nir Felder on guitar and Fima Ephron on bass. At 10 p.m., 55 Bar, 55 Christopher Street, West Village , (212) 929-9883, 55bar.com; cover, $10. (Chinen)20100114

MIKE STERN TRIO (Monday and Wednesday) A jazz-rock guitarist with an impeccable pedigree, Mike Stern never fails to find stalwart sidemen, like the bassist Richard Bona and the drummer Obed Calvaire, who join him here. At 10 p.m., 55 Bar, 55 Christopher Street, West Village , (212) 929-9883, 55bar.com; cover, $15; includes two drinks. (Chinen)20100114

STRYKER-SLAGLE BAND (Tuesday and Wednesday) The guitarist Dave Stryker and the alto saxophonist Steve Slagle have worked together in hard-driving settings since as far back as the mid-1980s. Rounding out this one are the bassist Jay Anderson and the drummer Victor Lewis. At 7:30 and 9:30 p.m., Jazz Standard, 116 East 27th Street, Manhattan , (212) 576-2232, jazzstandard.net; $25. (Chinen)20100114

DAVE VALENTIN WITH CANDIDO (Tuesday and Wednesday) Mr. Valentin, a charismatic and rhythmically assertive flutist with broad experience in Latin jazz, teams up here with Candido Camero, the legendary conga player, now approaching 90 but still capable of magnetism. At 8 and 10:30 p.m., Blue Note, 131 West Third Street, Greenwich Village , (212) 475-8592, bluenote.net; $25 at tables; $15 at the bar; with a $5 minimum. (Chinen) 20100114
