Criminal Past for Gunman in Las Vegas

  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints
By THE ASSOCIATED PRESS
Published: January 5, 2010

LAS VEGAS (AP) — The gunman who killed a courthouse security guard and wounded a deputy United States marshal on Monday before being fatally shot by federal officers had a lengthy criminal history in Tennessee and California, law enforcement officials said on Tuesday.

Kevin Favreau, the special agent in charge of the F.B.I.’s Las Vegas division, said the gunman, Johnny L. Wicks, 66, faced murder charges in Memphis in the mid-1970s and sexual assault charges in Sacramento in the late 1980s. The resolution of those charges was not immediately available.

Records show that Mr. Wicks lost a federal lawsuit last year challenging a cut in his Social Security benefits after he moved to Las Vegas from California.

The Federal Bureau of Investigation and the local police said Mr. Wicks set fire to his apartment early Monday and then, clad entirely in black, walked three miles to the Lloyd D. George Federal Courthouse in downtown Las Vegas. He opened fire in the lobby, before the entrance to the security area where visitors and their belongings pass through metal detectors and X-ray machines, officials said.

The security guard, Stanley W. Cooper, 65, a retired Las Vegas police officer, was killed instantly. The deputy marshal, whose identity was not made public, suffered a minor gunshot wound to the arm. He was released from the hospital, the authorities said on Tuesday.
