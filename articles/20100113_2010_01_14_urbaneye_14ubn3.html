The Hardest-Working Man in Publishing

  • Print
Published: January 13, 2010

Dave Eggers makes other writers feel lazy. When he’s not spinning out “great narrative nonfiction” in the form of “Zeitoun,” his chronicle of a man and his family brought down by Hurricane Katrina, he’s writing scripts, see “Where the Wild Things Are” and “Away We Go,” in between overseeing a one-time only, Sunday-edition-size newspaper: “McSweeney’s No. 33: The San Francisco Panorama” with full-color comics from Chris Ware, Dan Clowes and Art Spiegelman. Tell him to slow down at The Strand today where he’ll be appearing at 12. p.m.
