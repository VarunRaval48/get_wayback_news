Shopping With Grace Bonney

Sweet January

Christopher Smith for The New York Times
By JULIE SCELFO
Published: January 13, 2010
  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints

WELL-DESIGNED home furnishings aren’t hard to find, but too often, said Grace Bonney, 28, the founder of the popular blog Design*Sponge, they are priced beyond the reach of people like her.

Multimedia

Shopping With Grace Bonney

That was one reason she started her blog in 2004; it now gets 50,000 readers a day, Ms. Bonney said. “The things I thought were pretty” in magazines, she said, “were above my price point, and I was trying to find things I thought were pretty that I could afford on my just-out-of-college budget.”

One trick she uses, she said, is to scour the sales in January, when many stores — including the smaller ones that sell the handmade products she favors — offer significant discounts to clear their stock for spring.

Hunting for bargains at this year’s post-holiday sales, Ms. Bonney began at Nova Zembla in Brooklyn Heights, where she picked out a chocolate brown sofa marked down from $3,625 to $2,175 — a good value for 20-somethings setting up their first homes, she said.

“It’s a nice little mix of modern and traditional,” she said, pointing out the single seat cushion and the casters on the front legs. “My average reader is the same age that I am. As we all get a little older and think about things that will last a little longer, we’re becoming interested in more traditional or classic items that will stand the test of time.”

Around the corner at Abitare, Ms. Bonney admired Madeline Weinrib’s delicate cotton napkins, with hand-printed zigzags in camel, black or navy on a white background, priced at $8 each instead of $12.50.

Browsing through the clearance section at Urban Outfitters, a block away, she discovered playful plaques of woodsmen by Ashley G and Drew, for $9.99 each instead of $24. “Just 10 bucks,” she said, “and you’ve got something original and fun for your house.”

Later, in Manhattan, over lunch at Le Pain Quotidien near Union Square, Ms. Bonney explained her philosophy on shopping. Although she loves great design and finding a bargain, she said, she doesn’t spend a lot — or, for that matter, shop very often — because she would rather invest in a few quality pieces that will last: “I don’t honestly shop a lot. I spend a week obsessing over a rug or over a bench, and then I’m like, O.K., I’m done with it.”

Afterward, she walked up the block to Fishs Eddy, where she was drawn to a melamine tray with a bird design for $7.48, about half the regular price of $14.95.

But it was at Paul Smith that she saw what she thought was a better long-term investment: a white china tea set with colorful handles and rims. The cup and saucer set was $39, marked down from $55; the creamer was $35, originally $50. Without looking “silly or juvenile,” she said, the colored accents provide “that great mix of sophisticated and fun.”

JULIE SCELFO
