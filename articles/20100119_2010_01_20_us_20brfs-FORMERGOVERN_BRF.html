Iowa: Former Governor Seeks a Return

  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints
By THE ASSOCIATED PRESS
Published: January 19, 2010

Terry E. Branstad, a Republican who served four terms as governor of Iowa before leaving office in 1998, announced his bid to return to the state’s top job. He pledged to repair the state’s battered budget by cutting state spending by 15 percent while focusing on schools. Three other Republicans are seeking the nomination to challenge Gov. Chet Culver, and Democrats have begun attacking Mr. Branstad’s tenure, citing two increases in the state’s sales tax and an increase in the gasoline tax.
