Published: January 4, 2010

To the Editor:

Skip to next paragraph

RSS Feed

  • Get Science News From The New York Times »

“Down on the Farm, an Endless Cycle of Waste” (Dec. 29) would complete the cycle of life by returning the dairy manure to the grain fields where the cattle feed (corn and soybeans, among others) is grown. It is interesting to note this is the method farmers used for decades when cattle and grain fields were located on the same farm. Joseph Madlinger

Germantown, Tenn.

To the Editor:

In their attempts to confront the relentless production of animal waste products from farmed animals, ethical issues are not addressed by science and technology researchers.

The detrimental effects of animal agriculture on the environment, climate, human health and deforestation are expanding with increased worldwide consumption of meat. These effects can never be adequately addressed through technology.

Should not science have a broader vision that is connected to ethics?

Irene Muschel

New York
