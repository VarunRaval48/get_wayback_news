AT&T; Is the Latest to Drop Woods

By KEN BELSON
Published: December 31, 2009

Tiger Woods needs a brand-new bag.

AT&T, whose logo had been emblazoned on Woods’s golf bag, said Thursday that it was ending its sponsorship agreement with him. In a brief statement, the company said it wished him well in the future.

AT&T joins Accenture in severing its sponsorship deals with Woods after he acknowledged reports of marital infidelity and said he would take an “indefinite break” from professional golf to focus on his family.

Tag Heuer, the Swiss luxury watchmaker, Procter & Gamble and other companies have scaled back marketing campaigns that include Woods.

Nike, another major sponsor, has stood by Woods, whose personal life became a public spectacle after he crashed his SUV into a neighbor’s tree in the early morning of Nov. 27.

AT&T had earlier said it was evaluating its relationship with Woods, who was recently voted the PGA Tour player of the year by the players for the 10th time in his 13 years on the tour.

Unlike Nike, which sells Tiger Woods-branded apparel, AT&T was more focused on using him to boost its brand. That made it easier to justify cutting ties when his reputation took a hit, marketing experts said.

“These are companies affiliated with him for things not having to do with his core ability, which is playing golf,” said Paul Swangard, managing director of the Warsaw Sports Marketing Center at the University of Oregon School of Business. These sponsors “rely on the larger brand equity, the attributes beyond Tiger’s phenomenal athletic talent.”

AT&T, which has been in a long battle with Verizon Wireless for customers, has had its logo on Woods’s golf bag since February. The company replaced Buick, which ended its relationship with Woods because of the financial troubles at General Motors.

Swangard said that some companies may shy away from using individual athletes as pitchmen in the future, or may rely on former athletes. “For what you paid Tiger, you can probably sign three or four legends of golf,” he said.

AT&T is also the sponsor of the AT&T National tournament, which has been promoted as being “hosted by Tiger Woods.” Woods won last year’s tournament, held at the Congressional Country Club in Bethesda, Md.

The event raised millions of dollars for the Tiger Woods Foundation, including more than $2 million for the development of a Tiger Woods Learning Center near Washington.

Woods’s name and photos still adorn the Web site of the tournament, which will be held in late June. But Woods’s indefinite absence from the PGA Tour means that he will not host the event this year, according to Ty Votaw, a spokesman for the tour.

AT&T said it would continue to sponsor the tournament. But its sponsorship of Tiger Jam, an annual charity event held in Las Vegas to raise money for the Tiger Woods Foundation, has expired.

Over the years, Bon Jovi, Sting, Stevie Wonder and others have performed at the event. On his foundation’s Web site, Woods said that a date for the event in 2010 had not been determined.
