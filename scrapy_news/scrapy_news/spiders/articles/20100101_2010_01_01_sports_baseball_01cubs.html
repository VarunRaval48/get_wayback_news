With Bradley Out, Cubs Bring In Byrd

  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints
By THE ASSOCIATED PRESS
Published: December 31, 2009

CHICAGO (AP) — The Cubs, who had been searching for a center fielder, agreed Thursday to a three-year, $15 million contract with Marlon Byrd.

The Cubs are hoping to have more success with this former Texas Rangers outfielder than the one they signed last off-season, Milton Bradley.

Byrd, 32, hit .283 with 20 home runs and 89 runs batted in last season for Texas.

He will take over in center for Kosuke Fukudome, who will move to right field full time after the trade of Bradley to Seattle.

Byrd will be reunited with the former Texas and current Cubs hitting coach Rudy Jaramillo. In three seasons working with Jaramillo in Texas, Byrd batted .295 with 40 homers and 212 R.B.I.

Byrd will get $3 million next year, $5.5 million in 2011 and $6.5 million in 2012.

Bradley signed a three-year, $30 million deal with the Cubs and lasted one contentious season. He hit .257 with 40 R.B.I. and was suspended in the final two weeks of the season by General Manager Jim Hendry after criticizing the atmosphere on the Cubs.
