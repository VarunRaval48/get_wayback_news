2 Policemen Are Wounded as 3rd Officer Fires at Pit Bull

  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints
By SARAH WHEATON and AL BAKER
Published: January 5, 2010

Two plainclothes police officers chasing a suspect as part of a drug investigation in the Bronx were injured Tuesday night when a third officer fired at a pit bull that had charged at them and a bullet ricocheted, a Police Department spokesman said.

Skip to next paragraph
Enlarge This Image
Hiroko Masuike for The New York Times

A police car carried a pit bull that the police said was released from an apartment as officers were chasing after a suspect.

Bullet fragments grazed one of the officers in the face and struck the other in the leg, said the spokesman, Paul J. Browne. Neither injury was considered serious.

Both officers were taken to Jacobi Medical Center. “They appear to be conscious and O.K.,” Mr. Browne said from the scene about an hour after the shooting, adding that both were talking and in stable condition.

The officers were involved in a narcotics investigation on Davidson Avenue near West Fordham Road just after 8 p.m., the police said. Detective Thomas Guarino suffered a graze wound to his right cheek, and Detective Thomas Mchale had a graze wound to his left leg.

The police described a chain of events that began in front of a residential building on Davidson Avenue, where two other officers, both working undercover, went to look into complaints of drug sales. Those officers tried to buy drugs from a man in a car, but he refused to sell to them because he suspected that a van parked nearby was a police vehicle, the police said.

“One of them says that cops are around — ‘The block is hot. The block is hot,’ ” a police official said.

The undercover officers left the scene. Eight men who had been in front of the building then walked up to the van and looked inside, trying to discern if the occupants were police officers, the police said. The three plainclothes officers inside the van got out and identified themselves as officers, and the eight men fled in different directions, the police said.

One of the officers, a sergeant, pursued one man toward the west side of the street. The two others, joined by a third officer, followed another man down an alleyway, where, according to the police, the man opened an apartment door and released a pit bull.

One of the officers fired a single round, which ricocheted, and the shards struck the other two officers. The pit bull was also wounded in its front right paw.

The police took all eight men into custody, Mr. Browne said. Officers also closed part of Fordham Road, a bustling commercial thoroughfare.

“Narcotics work, including undercover work, is always dangerous,” Mr. Browne said, “particularly when people are willing to use weapons against the police, whether it’s in the form of a pit bull or something else.”

Mayor Michael R. Bloomberg and Police Commissioner Raymond W. Kelly met with the officers at the hospital, the police said.

Karen Zraick contributed reporting.
