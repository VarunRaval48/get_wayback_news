Tamara Duricka and Evan Johnson

  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints
By ROSALIE R. RADOMSKY
Published: January 16, 2010

Tamara Jane Duricka and Evan Blake Johnson were married Saturday morning at the Los Angeles Temple Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints in California. Dr. James C. Brown, an elder in the church and the doctor who delivered the bridegroom, performed the ceremony.

Skip to next paragraph

Ms. Duricka, 32, is taking her husband’s name. Until December, she worked in New York as a writer and producer for “Good Morning America” on ABC. She graduated from Roanoke College and received a master’s in journalism from Columbia.

She is the daughter of Patricia Kelly of Manhattan Beach, Calif., and the late John Duricka; she is the stepdaughter of the late Keith Kelly. Her mother is a deputy public information officer at the Los Angeles Superior Court. Her father was a photographer for The Associated Press.

Mr. Johnson, 28, works in Los Angeles as a market research analyst for MarketCast, which conducts marketing research for the film industry. He is pursuing a master’s degree in quantitative methods in the social sciences at Columbia. He graduated summa cum laude from Brigham Young University-Hawaii.

He is a son of Rhonda Miner Johnson of Roseville, Calif., and the late R. Kim Johnson. His mother retired as a first-grade teacher at Lydia Jackson Elementary School in Whittier, Calif. His father was a sales representative in the Los Angeles area for Hoshino U.S.A., a distributor of musical instruments.

The couple met in January 2009 when Mr. Johnson stepped in at the last minute as Date No. 6 during Ms. Duricka’s project — 31 dates in 31 days, which she blogged about and now hopes to turn into a book.

“I needed a gimmick,” said Ms. Duricka, who came up with the adventure for her 31st year to pull herself out of a funk from her dismal 30th year, during which she had two breakups and her dog died.

“I offered to pay for the dates,” she said. “Granted, there were guys who wouldn’t allow for that. I offered to pay $31, and made up a list of things we could do for $31 or less.”

The first date was Jan. 15, and Valentine’s Day marked date No. 31, which would be the second date for the man who had won the hearts of Ms. Duricka and many of her readers.

Since $31 goes only so far, most of the dates involved excursions that were free or fairly inexpensive. For date No. 6 she had acquired free tickets on Craigslist for a musical near Gramercy Park. The afternoon before the show, she realized that she had the time wrong, and soon learned that her date could not make it.

She then sent out an S O S to friends for a replacement, and one came through with Mr. Johnson, who had not read her blog but quickly caught up.

“I was a little more excited,” Mr. Johnson said, “when I saw how cute she was on her blog.”

He also said he thought the competition was pretty fierce.

They both found the musical “laughable,” Mr. Johnson recalled, but instead of walking out, they amused themselves by writing notes to each other.

After the show, Mr. Johnson suggested that they take a stroll on the Columbia University campus and sneak up on the roof of the Mudd Engineering Building for a view of the city, followed by hot chocolate nearby.

“It wasn’t anything magical,” Ms. Duricka said. “It was as if I had known him. I knew I liked him a lot. I was nervous. I knew I really liked him and didn’t want to get carried away.”

She had 24 dates to go, and was determined to be open-minded to the end.

As the field narrowed, Mr. Johnson topped her list each time and won the second date on Valentine’s Day. This time, spending way over her $31 budget, Ms. Duricka splurged for dinner and tickets to “Billy Elliot.” About 70 dates later, Mr. Johnson proposed. ROSALIE R. RADOMSKY
