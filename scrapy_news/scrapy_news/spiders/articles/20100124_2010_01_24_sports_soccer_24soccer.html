Chelsea Slips by Preston North End in F.A. Cup

  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints
By REUTERS
Published: January 23, 2010

Chelsea, the defending F.A. Cup champion, advanced to the fifth round with a 2-0 victory at Preston North End on Saturday, while Burnley of the Premier League lost at second-tier Reading, 1-0.

Wigan Athletic escaped with a 2-2 draw at League 2 Notts County after trailing by two goals, and Wolverhampton Wanderers needed a late equalizer to tie visiting Crystal Palace of the League Championship, 2-2.

Fulham had some tricky moments before winning at Accrington Stanley, 3-1, while the League Cup finalist Aston Villa had to work hard to advance with a 3-2 defeat of third-tier Brighton and Hove Albion at Villa Park.

Birmingham City stretched its unbeaten streak in all competitions to 15 with an impressive 2-1 win at Everton, and in another all top-flight clash, Portsmouth guaranteed some much-needed income with a 2-1 home victory over Sunderland.

ROBBEN SPARKS BAYERN Bayern Munich climbed to the top of the German Bundesliga after an Arjen Robben goal 12 minutes from the end of the game secured a 3-2 win at Werder Bremen.

Robben, Bayern’s Dutch wing, scored on a spectacular free kick three minutes after Hugo Almeida had tied the score for Bremen.

Bayern, which extended its winning streak to eight matches in all competitions, has 39 points from 19 games. Bayer Leverkusen and Schalke 04 trail by 1 point.

ROONEY SCORED FOUR The defending Premier League champion Manchester United climbed into first place for the first time since October after a four-goal blitz by Wayne Rooney sealed a 4-0 home victory over Hull City.

United moved above Chelsea and Arsenal with 50 points from 23 games. Arsenal has 48 points from 22 matches, and Chelsea also has 48 but has played two games fewer than United.

Rio Ferdinand returned to the center of United’s defense after being sidelined by a back injury since a 2-0 defeat by Liverpool in October, but was relatively untroubled as United dominated Hull City, which is third to last.

Rooney took just eight minutes to put United ahead, pouncing on a rebound for his 16th league goal of the season, before making sure of victory with three late strikes.

PRESSURE ON IVORY COAST The time has come for Ivory Coast’s talented generation to deliver when it faces Algeria in a clash between World Cup-bound teams in the quarterfinals of the African Cup of Nations on Sunday in Cabinda, Angola.

“This generation will be playing for high stakes in the coming week,” Coach Vahid Halilhodzic said. “We lose all or we win all. It’s money time.”

The Ivorians, who got off to a sluggish start with a goalless draw against Burkina Faso, found their form in a 3-1 defeat of Ghana, but Algeria may prove tougher.

Algeria qualified for the World Cup (where it is in a group with the United States, England and Slovenia) with a playoff win against the two-time African champion Egypt in November, a feat that made quite an impression on Halilhodzic.

Although Algeria slumped to a 3-0 defeat to Malawi in its tournament opener, Halilhodzic knows his team faces a tricky test.

“Algeria knocked out Egypt, that’s a big reference,” he said, referring to Algeria’s World Cup playoff win over Egypt. “They have a very compact midfield and a good defense.”

Should Ivory Coast win, it faces the possibility of a repeat of the 2008 semifinal against Egypt, which will be the favorite against Cameroon in Benguela on Monday after three straight wins in group play.

Injury-ravaged Ghana will once again have to rely on several players who won the FIFA Under-20 World Cup in October when its takes on hosts Angola on Sunday in Luanda.

The Black Stars have to make do without John Paintsil, John Mensah and Stephen Appiah, who missed the trip to Angola, and the playmaker Michael Essien of Chelsea who returned to England after injuring his knee.

Host Angola received a boost with word that its top striker, Flavio, would be available for the match, Coach Manuel José said on Saturday.

Flavio, who scored three goals in Angola’s first two games at the tournament, sat out its last group match against Algeria because of a hamstring injury.

Of the five World Cup-bound teams in Angola, Nigeria is expected to have the easiest ride in Lubango on Monday when it faces Zambia, which finished first in its group ahead of Cameroon.

RED BULLS TEAM IN N.P.S.L. The Red Bulls of Major League Soccer will this season field an under-23 team in the 32-team National Premier Soccer League, an amateur league that is considered to be the fourth tier of soccer in the United States after M.L.S., U.S.S.F. D2 and U.S.L. Second Division.

The Red Bulls will play in the N.P.S.L.’s Atlantic Division of the Northeast Conference with the Maine Sting, New Hampshire Rapids, New York Athletic Club, Morris County Colonials, Brooklyn Italians and Long Island Academy.

The Red Bulls’ squad will be composed of players from its 2009 Super-20 League national championship team. (NYT)
