Shaky Seats, Leaky Fluids

  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints
By SCOTT STURGIS
Published: January 21, 2010

FUEL pump troubles in BMWs and shaky seats in Mazdas are among the latest troubles cited in technical service bulletins.

The bulletins are compiled by alldatapro.com and offer automakers’ insights into recurring problems with various models. The bulletins, known as T.S.B.’s, are not recalls; they are information carmakers provide to dealer service departments and mechanics. Unless otherwise noted, the manufacturers do not offer payment assistance for these repairs beyond normal warranty coverage.

Alldata.com sells a more comprehensive version of the service bulletins to consumers. Here are a few recent examples:

BMW Some 2007-8 335i, 335xi, 535i and 535xi models with the twin-turbo 6-cylinder N54 engine may develop a fuel pump problem that results in extended cranking time when starting or the engine control system defaulting to fail-safe mode. In T.S.B. 130309 issued on Nov. 1, BMW said it would extend the fuel-pump warranty on those cars to 10 years or 120,000 miles, from four years or 50,000 miles.

CHRYSLER Some 2009 Chrysler 300 and 300C and Dodge Charger and Challenger models may have trouble with their battery ground cables. In T.S.B. J21 issued on Nov. 11, Chrysler said about 15,500 of the vehicles with 5.7- or 6.1-liter engines may need a new battery ground cable and an upgraded battery. The problem could cause long cranking time when starting a warm engine.

FORD Some 2007-9 Ford Edge and Lincoln MKX crossovers may have trouble with corroding heater cores. In T.S.B. 09M04 issued on Nov. 10, Ford said owners of affected vehicles were entitled to a cooling system flush and refill and a warranty extension on the heater core to six years or 105,000 miles from four years or 50,000 miles. Affected vehicles may have poor heater performance.

Also, some 2010 Ford Taurus, Edge and Flex models may develop a transmission fluid leak. In T.S.B. 09-23-9 issued on Nov. 30, Ford said the leak might be caused by a bad power transfer unit.

Some 2005-10 Mustangs may emit a whine from the rear axle. In T.S.B. 09-20-4 issued on Oct. 19, Ford said the noise could be corrected by installing dampers on the rear axle assembly.

GENERAL MOTORS Some large 2009 S.U.V.’s may have a weak heater hose quick-connection, leading to a coolant leak. In T.S.B. 09269 issued on Oct. 2, G.M. said the connection might fail on Cadillac Escalade and Escalade ESV; Chevrolet Suburban and Tahoe; and GMC Yukon and Yukon XL models. Making sure the connection is pushed in until it clicks is enough to solve the problem.

Also, some 2010 Buick LaCrosse sedans may need their shift cables inspected. In T.S.B. 09279 issued on Oct. 16, G.M. said the transmission cable clip might not be fully engaged and could come loose, leaving a vehicle stuck in Park after the shifter is moved. Service departments are asked to check the connection and correct it if it is loose.

MAZDA Shaky seats may be rattling Mazda owners. In T.S.B. 0906109 issued on Oct. 23, Mazda said the trouble might occur during braking in these vehicles: 2003-10 Mazda 6; 2004-10 Mazda 3 and RX-8; 2006-10 Mazda 5; and 2007-10 CX-7 and CX-9. Installing three springs on the back of the seat cushion pan and frame should stop the rocking.

SCION Some 2008-9 Scion xDs may emit a noise and leak coolant from the water pump area. T.S.B. SB0048-09 issued on Oct. 12 said the trouble indicated a water pump gone bad.

TOYOTA Some Toyota models may exhibit intermittent foul odors from the climate control system. Toyota has issued three T.S.B.’s regarding this problem for three separate models. T.S.B. SB0378-09 issued on Nov. 10 applies to the 2006-9 RAV4; SB0379-09 also issued on Nov. 10 mentions the 2007-9 FJ Cruiser; and SB0384-009 issued on Nov. 19 notes the 2009-10 Corolla. A new air-conditioner evaporator subassembly corrects the issue.

VOLVO Some 2003-5 Volvo XC90 models may make a humming noise from the front brakes when the pedal is pressed lightly or not at all. T.S.B. TJ21108 issued on Oct. 5 said a damper on vehicles with single-piston calipers should clear up the problem.

Also, some 2007-10 XC90s may give off a harsh noise or vibration at low r.p.m. and high load. In T.S.B. TJ22050 issued on Nov. 25, Volvo said the cause could be a lack of bushings in the front exhaust mounting bracket. A redesigned bracket including bushings should quiet things down.
