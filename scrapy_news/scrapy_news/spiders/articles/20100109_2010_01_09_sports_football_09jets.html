Jets Offense Is Fortified by Five Big Men

  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints
By DAVE CALDWELL
Published: January 8, 2010

FLORHAM PARK, N.J. — What may look like a lot of pushing and shoving at the line of scrimmage, Jets center Nick Mangold said, is really an exercise of exquisite timing and footwork. The Jets’ offensive line, an aggregate 1,555 pounds, might as well be the Rockettes.

The Jets' offensive line has started 32 games in a row together, the longest streak in the league.

“To sit back and think where we were and where we are now, it’s a huge difference,” Mangold said this week as the Jets prepared for American Football Conference wild-card playoff game in Cincinnati on Saturday against the Bengals.

This offensive line was assembled 22 months ago — an eternity for a such a unit — when Damien Woody, a free-agent right tackle, joined another free agent, left guard Alan Faneca, left tackle D’Brickashaw Ferguson, right guard Brandon Moore and Mangold.

These five men have started the last 32 games, the longest current streak among N.F.L. offensive lines. They helped the Jets accumulate 2,756 rushing yards in the regular season, the most of any team in the league.

They also helped the Jets overcome two potentially critical shortcomings: the loss of the running back Leon Washington, who broke his leg Oct. 25 in a 38-0 victory against the Oakland Raiders; and the relative inexperience of the rookie quarterback Mark Sanchez, who needed the security blanket that the line provided.

“We were brought in here piece by piece for this moment, for this situation,” Woody said. “We look at ourselves as a rock, not just for ourselves, but for the team.”

All five linemen but Moore are former first-round draft picks. Mangold, Faneca and Ferguson are going to the Pro Bowl. Faneca, a 12-year veteran, will start his 12th playoff game. Woody will start his fifth. Moore has started 89 consecutive regular-season games and Ferguson 64.

That is a lot of talent and experience. But many times in football history, talented and experienced players have failed to jell. Moore, the lightest of the group with Mangold at 305 pounds, said this group came together quickly and was getting better.

“They don’t give out awards to offensive linemen, but this is the best group of guys I’ve been around,” said Moore, who is in his seventh season. “This is a great group. Every guy in this line is a playmaker. You don’t get that too often.”

The line has enabled the Jets’ first-year coach, Rex Ryan, to use an old-fashioned ground-and-pound philosophy, in which the Jets seek to wear out opponents with a withering rushing attack.

Running back Thomas Jones, who amassed a career-high 1,402 yards, said this week that opponents sometimes loaded as many as 10 defenders near the line of scrimmage to stop the run.

“Whatever we see on Saturday, it won’t be anything we haven’t seen before,” Jones said.

The line has meshed so well, Mangold said, that the five players can anticipate the adjustments each will make during a play that might not develop the way it is drawn. “It’s an intuitive thing,” Mangold said.

The line has lightened the load on Sanchez, the only rookie quarterback in the playoffs. Ryan, the offensive coordinator Brian Schottenheimer and the offensive line coach Bill Callahan set a goal before the season to lead the league in rushing.

According to Schottenheimer, Moore said then that he thought Schottenheimer did not really believe the Jets could accomplish that. But they did. “I think they’re the best in football,” Schottenheimer said, referring to the offensive line.

Is it the best he has ever been around? “No question, yes,” Schottenheimer said.

Schottenheimer said: “We’ve always said that it takes a couple of games to get the running game going. That’s kind of what you saw. You fit different pieces in, and we’ve changed some things that we did with the way that we were going to read some things with the backs. It takes some time.”

He added: ”Have there been some hiccups? Sure, but it’s been the thing we’ve hung our hat on all year.”

It is expected to be cold in Cincinnati, perhaps nearly as cold as it was when the Jets beat the Bengals, 37-0, in their regular-season finale Sunday. The Bengals will be fortified with the addition of three defensive starters who did not play Sunday.

The Jets sound as if they will be running the ball anyway.

Ryan drew laughs Wednesday when he said that the line was not good until he arrived. “I stepped up and I’m in charge of the O-line,” he said. “I know Callahan gets the credit. It’s really me. I give Bill a lot of things that you guys don’t see behind the scenes. That’s where I do my coaching.”

Ryan smiled and said: “It’s a great group. That’s why I’m set up to succeed. We have the players. We have great coaches. We have the organization from top to bottom.”
