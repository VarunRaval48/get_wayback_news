Roddick Beats Stepanek to Win Brisbane Warm-Up for Australian Open

  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
By THE ASSOCIATED PRESS
Published: January 10, 2010

BRISBANE, AUSTRALIA — Top-seeded Andy Roddick survived a second-set collapse Sunday to win the Brisbane International title, beating the defending champion, Radek Stepanek, who double-faulted on match point.

Roddick’s 7-6 (7-2), 7-6 (9-7) victory in the final capped his first tournament in nearly three months after an injury ended his season last year. Roddick hurt his left knee in October, and Brisbane, a warm-up for the Australian Open, was his first tournament back.

Roddick nearly let the match slip away. He led 4-0 in the second set before Stepanek came back to take a 6-5 lead, with Roddick needing to hold service to force a tie breaker.

Roddick dominated early in the tie breaker, but Stepanek came from 6-1 down to make it close.

Stepanek, a Czech, held a set point at 7-6 in the second-set tie breaker. Roddick, an American, won the next two points on serve, then watched Stepanek double-fault to hand him the title.

The lopsided tie breaker loss in the first set seemed to deflate Stepanek. Serving to open the second set, he hit a forehand into the net, double-faulted, misfired on a backhand and double-faulted again on break point to give Roddick a 1-0 lead.

Roddick led 4-0 before Stepanek broke his powerful serve twice to level the set at 5-5.

The women’s singles champion was decided Saturday when Kim Clijsters beat her fellow Belgian Justine Henin by 6-3, 4-6, 7-6 (8-6) in a clash of two players back on tour after temporary retirements.

Clijsters, only five tournaments into her own comeback, which has already netted the U.S. Open title, saved two match points and then wasted three before clinching the victory over Henin, a seven-time Grand Slam title winner playing her first tournament back on tour.

Henin, 27, later pulled out of the Sydney International this week with an upper left leg injury, but she expects to be fit for the Australian Open, which starts on Jan. 18.

Henin said she strained her upper left leg earlier in the week and aggravated it in the final.

“It was a bit sore in the last few days and really got worse today in the third set,” she said. “I hope it’s not that serious and that I’m going to be able to recover soon, but it would be just a great, great mistake to go and play in Sydney.”

Henin said she would consult a doctor again if it had not improved within a couple of days.

“It was very tight for a few days, but it really got worse in the end,” she said. “It’s no excuse, it’s not the reason” for the loss to Clijsters.

Henin has no ranking after quitting the tour in May 2008, when she held the No. 1 ranking. She received wild-card entries to play in Brisbane and Sydney ahead of the Australian Open, her first major since she lost in the quarterfinals there in 2008.

Spain has won its third Hopman Cup title by defeating Britain, The Associated Press reported from Perth, Australia.

In the deciding match Saturday, Tommy Robredo and María José Martínez Sánchez combined to beat Andy Murray and Laura Robson by 7-6 (8-6), 7-5 in mixed doubles. It was the first loss of the week for Murray and Robson in mixed doubles.

Spain twice went up a break in the first set, but Britain broke back both times in the next game, including when Martínez Sánchez was serving for the set at 6-5. In the tie breaker, Spain saved three set points and won five points in a row over all.

In the deciding set, Spain broke Robson’s serve to win the trophy, a diamond-encrusted silver tennis ball.

Earlier in the Spain-Britain match, Robson won her first singles match of the week, by 6-1, 7-6 (6) over Martínez Sánchez, to give Britain an early 1-0 lead.

Robson, the 2008 junior Wimbledon champion, led her singles match by 6-2 in the second-set tie breaker but needed five match points to clinch it.

“I was pretty nervous at the end, but I just kept trying to go for my shots,” Robson said. “I played pretty well, particularly in the first set.”

Robredo then tied the final by winning his fourth straight singles match of the tournament, 1-6, 6-4, 6-3, to hand Murray his first loss in singles. Murray converted just two of 11 break points and committed 29 unforced errors.

“I’m so happy because Andy is one of the great players in the world right now and beating him at the beginning of the year is going to give me a lot of confidence,” Robredo said. “After 6-1 for Andy, he was playing amazing and I was only hoping to keep playing to enjoy the match. I just tried my best and kept going.

“I was just watching the diamond ball and I was pushing hard because I really would love to have one more.”

Robredo won the event in 2002, when his partner was Arantxa Sánchez Vicario.

Britain was playing in the Hopman Cup final for the first time, thanks to Murray’s winning all his matches comfortably to compensate for the losses of the inexperienced Robson in group meetings with Russia, Germany and Kazakhstan.

Spain was in its fifth final. The women’s singles result Saturday was its first dropped match, after sweeping the group-stage matchups with Australia, the United States and Romania.
