    ‘Avatar’ on Top Again

    Compiled by RACHEL LEE HARRISJAN. 3, 2010

    Inside
      Supported by

      Advertisement

      Continue reading the main story
      Continue reading the main story Share This Page
      Continue reading the main story
      Continue reading the main story

      Critics may be at odds about the meaning of Fox’s 3-D science-fiction epic “Avatar,” but its meaning to the box office is clear: It started the new year at No. 1 in domestic ticket sales. With just over $68 million for the weekend, “Avatar,” directed by James Cameron, left (in 3-D specs), finished its first 17 days in theaters with $352.1 million, a record for a film in its third week of release, according to Variety. Robert Downey Jr. and Jude Law helped keep “Sherlock Holmes” (Warner Brothers) at No. 2 with a little more than $38 million, bringing its total to about $140.7 million. “Alvin and the Chipmunks: The Squeakquel” (Fox) remained at No. 3 in its second week, with $36 million ($157 million, total). Nancy Meyers’s romantic comedy “It’s Complicated” (Universal) finished with $18 million, putting it in fourth place with about $60 million total. And though Sandra Bullock was the top box office draw of 2009, “The Blind Side” (Warner Brothers) drew only a little more than $12 million in its seventh week in theaters. Still, it was enough to tip it over the $200 million mark, with $209 million total.
