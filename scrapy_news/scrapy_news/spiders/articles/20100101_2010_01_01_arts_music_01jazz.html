Published: December 30, 2009

JAZZ

THE BAD PLUS (Friday through Sunday) The pianist Ethan Iverson, the bassist Reid Anderson and the drummer David King constitute the Bad Plus, a delivery system for smartly sweeping original songs, as well as wryly affectionate renditions of pop tunes. At 9 and 11 p.m., Village Vanguard, 178 Seventh Avenue South, at 11th Street, West Village , (212) 255-4037, villagevanguard.com; cover, $25, with a $10 minimum. 20091231

(Nate Chinen)

BAD TOUCH (Thursday) Bad Touch — not to be confused with the Bad Plus, above — is a smart young band that strives toward a single metabolism, with contributions from the alto saxophonist Loren Stillman, the guitarist Nate Radley, the organist Gary Versace and the drummer Ted Poor. At 8 p.m., the Stone, Avenue C and Second Street, East Village , thestonenyc.com; cover, $10. 20091231

(Chinen)

KENNY BARRON QUINTET (Thursday) Kenny Barron is the leading proponent of an elegant and economical piano style that thrives in any mainstream setting. He teams up here with the flutist Anne Drummond, the vibraphonist Stefon Harris, the bassist Kiyoshi Kitagawa and the drummer Ben Riley. (Through Jan. 10.) At 7:30 and 9:30 p.m., Jazz Standard, 116 East 27th Street, Manhattan , (212) 576-2232, jazzstandard.net; cover, $30. (Chinen)20091231

TIM BERNE AND LOS TOTOPOS (Thursday) Mr. Berne, an alto saxophonist and composer with a taste for coarsely layered frictions, presents a newly minted ensemble with Matt Mitchell on piano and electronics, Oscar Noriega on clarinets and Ches Smith on percussion. The band focuses not only on sharp and convoluted new music by Mr. Berne, but also on some rather obscure material written more than 30 years ago by his former mentor, the saxophonist and composer Julius Hemphill. At 9 and 10:30 p.m., Jazz Gallery, 290 Hudson Street, at Spring Street, South Village , (212) 242-1063, jazzgallery.org; cover, $15. (Chinen)20091231

SEAMUS BLAKE QUINTET (Tuesday and Wednesday) Mr. Blake is a tenor and soprano saxophonist with a sleekly modern style, and his quintet features smart and sympathetic partners: the pianist David Kikoski, the guitarist Lage Lund, the bassist Matt Clohesy and the drummer Bill Stewart. At 9 and 10:30 p.m., Smalls, 183 West 10th Street, West Village , (212) 252-5091, smallsjazzclub.com; cover, $20. (Chinen)20091231

MARC CARY FOCUS TRIO (Friday and Saturday) Mr. Cary approaches the piano-trio tradition from a predominantly rhythmic standpoint, but with evidence of harmonic sophistication. As he does on his most recent album — “Focus” (Motema), released in 2006 — he works here with David Ewell on bass and Sameer Gupta on drums. At 8, 10 and 11:30 p.m., Smoke, 2751 Broadway, at 106th Street , (212) 864-6662, smokejazz.com; cover, $30. (Chinen)20091231

★ CLAYTON BROTHERS QUINTET (Wednesday and Thursday) John Clayton, a bassist, and Jeff Clayton, a saxophonist, have been sharing bandstands for more than 30 years, and their rapport, captured on a recent album called “Brother to Brother” (ArtistShare), feels like a stout and uncomplicated thing. In this band they enlist the canny young pianist Gerald Clayton (John’s son), as well as the trumpeter Terell Stafford and the drummer Obed Calvaire. (Through Jan. 12.) At 7:30 and 9:30 p.m., Dizzy’s Club Coca-Cola, Frederick P. Rose Hall, Jazz at Lincoln Center, 60th Street and Broadway , (212) 258-9595, jalc.org; cover, $30, with a minimum of $10 at tables, $5 at the bar. (Chinen)20091231

RAVI COLTRANE QUARTET (Wednesday and Thursday) As he has for years now, Ravi Coltrane balances his slippery, thoughtful tenor and soprano saxophone playing against the cohesive stir of a rhythm section with the pianist Luis Perdomo, the bassist Drew Gress and the drummer E. J. Strickland. (Through Jan. 10.) At 8:30 and 10:30 p.m., Iridium, 1650 Broadway, at 51st Street , (212) 582-2121, iridiumjazzclub.com; cover, $35, with a $10 minimum. (Chinen)20091231

ADAM CRUZ GROUP (Tuesday) An assertive and adaptable drummer, Mr. Cruz takes a turn in the bandleader’s seat, with strong support: Mark Turner on tenor saxophone, Will Vinson on alto saxophone, Steve Cardenas on guitar, Aaron Goldberg on Fender Rhodes piano and Joe Martin on bass. At 10 p.m., 55 Bar, 55 Christopher Street, West Village , (212) 929-9883, 55bar.com; cover, $10. (Chinen)20091231

JACK DEJOHNETTE QUINTET (Tuesday through Thursday) Mr. DeJohnette, one of the loosest and most inventive drummers in jazz, leads a fascinating assortment of strong personalities in this fusioneering quintet: the alto saxophonist Rudresh Mahanthappa, the guitarist David Fiuczynski, the keyboardist George Colligan and the bassist Jerome Harris. (Through Jan. 9.) At 8:30 and 11 p.m., Birdland, 315 West 44th Street, Clinton , (212) 581-3080, birdlandjazz.com; cover, $30 and $40, with a $10 minimum. (Chinen)20091231

PETER EVANS SEXTET (Monday) Mr. Evans, probably best recognized for his role in the upstart free-bop quartet Mostly Other People Do the Killing, is a trumpeter with an expressive command of timbre and tone. He’s also a bandleader of emerging promise; in this group he corrals the pianist Carlos Homs, the bassist Tom Blancarte, the drummers Kassa Overall and Kevin Shea, and the electronics artist Sam Pluta. An opening set will feature solo saxophone improvisations by Johnny Butler. At 10 p.m., Zebulon, 258 Wythe Avenue, near Metropolitan Avenue, Williamsburg, Brooklyn , (718) 218-6934, zebuloncafeconcert.com; no cover. (Chinen)20091231

GEORGE GARZONE GROUP (Wednesday) Mr. Garzone, a tenor saxophonist with a headlong but intelligent approach to free improvisation, presides over a tribute to the pianist Charlie Banacos, an influential jazz educator who traveled in the same Boston circles as Mr. Garzone (and who died just last month). The ensemble gathered for the task is worthy, with Rachel Z on piano, Peter Slavov on bass and Pete Zimmer on drums. At 8:30 p.m., Cornelia Street Café, 29 Cornelia Street, West Village , (212) 989-9319, corneliastreetcafe.com; cover, $10, with a one-drink minimum. (Chinen)20091231

KATT HERNANDEZ AND JOE MORRIS (Thursday) Mr. Morris, a guitarist who applies the clear tone of Jim Hall to darker purposes, has an assertive partner here in Ms. Hernandez, a Philadelphia-based violinist with broad experience in free-improvising and psychedelic-folk circles. At 10 p.m., the Stone, Avenue C and Second Street, East Village , thestonenyc.com; cover, $10. (Chinen)20091231

ARI HOENIG GROUP (Monday and Thursday) Mr. Hoenig approaches jazz drumming with manic glee, and yet he’s nothing if not exacting about his craft. His fine working band includes players of similar temperament: the guitarist Mike Moreno, the pianist Tigran Hamasyan and the bassist Orlando Le Fleming. Monday at 9 and 10:30 p.m., Smalls, 183 West 10th Street, West Village, (212) 252-5091, smallsjazzclub.com; cover, $20. Thursday at 8:30 p.m., Cornelia Street Café, 29 Cornelia Street, West Village , (212) 989-9319, corneliastreetcafe.com; cover, $15, with a one-drink minimum. (Chinen)20091231

CHARLIE HUNTER (Tuesday) On his fantastically titled new album, “Gentlemen, I Neglected to Inform You You Will Not Be Getting Paid” (Spire Artist Media), the eight-stringed guitarist Charlie Hunter renews his investment in backbeat funk and instrumental gospel groove, with a cohort that includes a few horns. Here he begins a monthlong Tuesday-night residency in Williamsburg, Brooklyn, with two musicians from the album: the trombonist Curtis Fowlkes and the drummer Eric Kalb. At 9 and 11 p.m., Rose Live Music, 345 Grand Street, Williamsburg, Brooklyn , (718) 599-0069, liveatrose.com; $10 in advance, $12 at the door, $10 with a student ID. (Chinen)20091231

ANDY LASTER’S CAST A SPELL TRIO (Saturday) Mr. Laster is a multireedist and composer drawn to complex but graceful convolutions. His Cast a Spell Trio, with the pianist Mick Rossi and the percussionist John Mettam, plays music written for a play of the same name by Traci Parks. (Some new originals will also turn up.) At 10 p.m., the Stone, Avenue C and Second Street, East Village , thestonenyc.com; cover, $10. 20091231

(Chinen)

MAT MANERI QUARTET (Tuesday) Mr. Maneri, a violist with an elastic approach to pulse and pitch, leads a well-suited band with Jacob Sacks on piano, Garth Stevenson on bass and Randy Peterson on drums. At 7 p.m., Barbès, 376 Ninth Street, at Sixth Avenue, Park Slope, Brooklyn , (347) 422-0248, barbesbrooklyn.com; cover, $10. (Chinen)20091231

NICOLE MITCHELL’S TRUTH OR DARE (Saturday) More than a serious and soulful flutist, Ms. Mitchell, from Chicago, organizes her music with a high degree of conceptual savvy. In this group, which probably has nothing to do with Madonna (though you never know), she works alongside the violinist Renée Baker and the drummer Shirazette Tinnin. At 8 p.m., the Stone, Avenue C and Second Street, East Village , thestonenyc.com; cover, $10. (Chinen)20091231

THOMAS MORGAN AND DAN WEISS (Sunday) Mr. Morgan, a bassist, and Mr. Weiss, a drummer, have become one of the stalwart young rhythm teams in New York jazz circles, a twin turbine of shifting texture and forward motion. This duo performance seems likely to destabilize the usual background-to-foreground ratio, in lively and provocative ways. At 10 p.m., the Stone, Avenue C and Second Street, East Village , thestonenyc.com; cover, $10. (Chinen)20091231

★ MARK O’CONNOR, JOHN PATITUCCI, JULIAN LAGE (Thursday) Western and gypsy swing are just two overlapping areas of inquiry likely to surface during this first-time encounter between Mr. O’Connor, the celebrated fiddler; Mr. Patitucci, a virtuoso bassist; and Mr. Lage, a bright-eyed and irrepressible young guitarist. The results are sure to be buoyant; they may also be dazzling. (Through Jan. 9.) At 8 and 10:30 p.m. Blue Note, 131 West Third Street, Greenwich Village , (212) 475-8592, bluenote.net; cover, $30 at tables, $20 at the bar, with a $5 minimum. (Chinen)20091231

JESSICA PAVONE’S ARMY OF STRANGERS (Tuesday) Ms. Pavone, a violist and violinist with an increasingly prominent profile in avant-garde circles, works here with musicians a good deal more acquainted than her project’s name would suggest: Brandon Seabrook on guitar, Jonti Siman on bass and Harris Eisenstadt on drums. At 8 p.m., the Stone, Avenue C and Second Street, East Village , thestonenyc.com; cover, $10.20091231

(Chinen)

★ BEN PEROWSKY QUARTET (Wednesday) The aggressive but nimble drummer Ben Perowsky draws here from a very good recent album, “Esopus Opus” (Skirl), with several reliable partners: the saxophonist and clarinetist Chris Speed, the accordionist Ted Reichman and the bassist Drew Gress. At 8 p.m., Barbès, 376 Ninth Street, at Sixth Avenue, Park Slope, Brooklyn , (347) 422-0248, barbesbrooklyn.com; cover, $10. (Chinen)20091231

CHRIS POTTER’S UNDERGROUND (Tuesday through Thursday) The saxophonist Chris Potter has an improvisational approach that’s intellectual and athletic in equal measure, and he pushes it to the fore with Underground, a turbocharged jazz-rock quartet that spotlights the shared exertions of Adam Rogers on guitar, Craig Taborn on Fender Rhodes piano, and Nate Smith on drums. (Through Jan. 10.) At 9 and 11 p.m., Village Vanguard, 178 Seventh Avenue South, at 11th Street, West Village , (212) 255-4037, villagevanguard.com; cover, $20, with a $10 minimum. (Chinen)20091231

WALLACE RONEY QUINTET (Sunday) “Jazz” (HighNote), the most recent release by the trumpeter Wallace Roney, documents a fierce and free-spirited postbop band, a version of which he leads here, with Antoine Roney (his brother) on saxophones, Aruán Ortiz on keyboards, Rashaan Carter on bass and Kush Abadey on drums. At 10:30 p.m., Le Poisson Rouge, 158 Bleecker Street, near Thompson Street, Greenwich Village , (212) 796-0741, lepoissonrouge.com; $15 in advance, $18 on Sunday, free for members. (Chinen)20091231

JOSH ROSEMAN’S EXTENDED CONSTELLATIONS (Friday) Mr. Roseman is a trombonist given to emphatic solo commentary, but he also enjoys the immersive depths of a large ensemble. This one includes the trumpeters Shane Endsley and Taylor Haskins; the trombonists Curtis Hasselbring and Ryan Snow; the saxophonists Andrew D’Angelo and Marcus Strickland; and a rhythm section with Nir Felder on guitar, Barney McAll on keyboards, Todd Sickafoose on bass and Dan Weiss on drums. At 9 and 10 p.m., Tea Lounge, 837 Union Street, near Sixth Avenue, Park Slope, Brooklyn , (718) 789-2762, tealoungeny.com; suggested donation, $5. (Chinen)20091231

★ STRUTTIN’ WITH SOME BARBECUE (Friday through Sunday) Here’s an irresistible proposition, at least for devotees of meaty groove. Henry Butler, a rollicking pianist, and Donald Harrison, a powerful alto saxophonist, both hail from New Orleans, while their partners in this assembled band — the trumpeter Sean Jones, the trombonist Wycliffe Gordon, the bassist Ben Wolfe and the drummer Ali Jackson — have earned their familiarity with the city’s native style. At 7:30 and 9:30 p.m., Jazz Standard, 116 East 27th Street, Manhattan , (212) 576-2232, jazzstandard.net; cover, $35. (Chinen)20091231

★ TWO SIDES OF DON BYRON (Sunday) Jazz has many card-carrying eclectics these days, but none more self-assured than the clarinetist Don Byron, who takes a highly serious approach to his multifaceted talent. This aptly named concert will begin with a solo recital by the classical pianist Lisa Moore, who recently released “Seven” (Cantaloupe), a digital EP of études by Mr. Byron. Then out comes the composer himself, leading a shrewdly rugged combo with George Colligan on piano, Kenny Davis on bass and Billy Hart on drums. At 7 p.m., Le Poisson Rouge, 158 Bleecker Street, near Thompson Street, Greenwich Village , (212) 796-0741, lepoissonrouge.com; $15 in advance, $18 on Sunday. (Chinen)20091231

DOUG WAMBLE (Monday) Mr. Wamble specializes in a rustic Southern sound, from the bluesy cry of his vocals to the quavering twang of his acoustic guitar. He’s a magnetic performer even by himself; in a working band like the one he leads here, with Rich Hinman on pedal-steel guitar and Jeff Hanley on bass, he can be gripping. At 8:30 and 10:30 p.m., Bar Next Door, 129 Macdougal Street, West Village , (212) 529-5945, lalanternacaffe.com; cover, $12, with a one-drink minimum. (Chinen)20091231
