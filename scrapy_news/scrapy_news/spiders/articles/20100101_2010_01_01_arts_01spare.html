Museums and Sites

AMERICAN MUSEUM OF NATURAL HISTORY Tuesday at 6:30 p.m., Brian Abbott, an astrophysicist, leads viewers through a screening of “The Known Universe” inside the Hayden Planetarium Space Theater. The short film takes viewers from the Himalayas through Earth’s atmosphere and the inky black of space to the afterglow of the Big Bang. Every star, planet and quasar seen in the film is possible because of the world’s most complete four-dimensional map of the universe, the Digital Universe Atlas. Through Sunday, the Origami Holiday Tree will be on display. Through Sunday, “Frogs: A Chorus of Colors,” with more than 200 live frogs representing 25 species. Through May 31, “The Butterfly Conservatory: Tropical Butterflies Alive in Winter,” featuring hundreds of live specimens. Through Aug. 15, “Traveling the Silk Road: Ancient Pathway to the Modern World.” In the Imax theater through Sunday, playing alternately every hour on the half-hour, from 10:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m., “Mysteries of the Great Lakes,” exploring its history and scenery, and “Beavers,” about the obstacles to building a dam. Timed tickets to “Frogs: A Chorus of Colors” and “The Butterfly Conservatory,” as well as tickets to Imax films, include museum admission: $24; $18 for students and 60+; $14 for ages 2 to 12; $12 for members and $7.50 for their children. Suggested general admission: $16; $12 for students and 60+; $9 for 2 to 12. Supersaver tickets, including museum and Rose Center admission, the Hayden Planetarium space show (“Journey to the Stars”) and all special exhibitions: $32; $24.50 for students and 60+; $20 for 2 to 12 (members, $16 and $11.50). Hours: daily, 10 a.m. to 5:45 p.m. Central Park West and 79th Street, (212) 769-5200, amnh.org.

NEW YORK PUBLIC LIBRARY Through June 26, “Mapping New York’s Shoreline,” an exhibition of maps, books, journals, prints and other items, in celebration of the 400th anniversary of Henry Hudson’s voyage. Mondays, and Thursdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m.; Tuesdays and Wednesdays, 10 a.m. to 9 p.m. Stephen A. Schwarzman Building, Fifth Avenue, at 42nd Street, (212) 275-6975, nypl.org.

NEW YORK TRANSIT MUSEUM Through Jan. 18, the eighth annual Grand Central Holiday Train Show, with cars representing the Long Island Rail Road and Metro North, as well as the New York City subway system. Monday through Wednesday, 8 a.m. to 8 p.m.; Saturday and Sunday, 10 a.m. to 7 p.m.; Gallery Annex, Grand Central Terminal, near the Stationmaster’s Office, (212) 878-0106, mta.info/mta/museum; free.

SOUTH STREET SEAPORT MUSEUM Through Sunday, “New Amsterdam: The Island at the Center of the World,” an exhibition on the early history of Manhattan, with rare documents, including Henry Hudson’s contract with the Dutch East India Company and a 1660 street plan of Lower Manhattan. Fridays through Sundays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. 12 Fulton Street, Lower Manhattan, (212) 748-8786, southstreetseaportmuseum.org; $12, $10 for students and 65+; $8 for those 5 to 12; free for members and for those under 5.

Gardens

NEW YORK BOTANICAL GARDEN Through Jan. 10, the Holiday Train Show, featuring a display of New York City landmarks. Holiday hours and pricing: Friday through Sunday, 10 a.m. to 7 p.m.; $25, $22 for 65+, $15 for children; Monday through Thursday, to 6 p.m.; $20, $18, $10. Free for children under 2 all days. Bronx River Parkway (Exit 7W) Fordham Road, Bedford Park, the Bronx, (718) 817-8700, nybg.org; Grounds admission only: $6; $5 for Bronx residents; $3 for 62+ and students; $1 for children 2 to 12; parking, $12.

Events

ASPHALT ORCHESTRA Thursday at 8:30 p.m., a radical 12-member street band brings rock, jazz and classical players together at the David Rubenstein Atrium in Lincoln Center; songs by Björk, Zappa and Mingus, as played by an ensemble including Stew, Tyondai Braxton of Battles and Goran Bregovic. Seating is limited and available on a first-come-first-served basis. Broadway at 62nd Street, (212) 721-6500, lincolncenter.org, asphaltorchestra.com; free.

‘LEONARDO DA VINCI’S WORKSHOP’ Through March 14, an interactive exhibition featuring life-size models of his inventions, including flying machines, robots and other mechanical devices. Daily, 10 a.m. to 10 p.m.; Discovery TSX, 226 West 44th Street, Midtown, (866) 987-9692, discoverytsx.com; $19.50, $18.50 for 65+, $17.50 for children 4 to 12.

ROBERT FROST’S ANNUAL CHRISTMAS CARDS Through Jan. 16, an exhibition of holiday chapbooks featuring poetry by Frost. Open Tuesday through Thursday, 11 a.m. to 7 p.m.; Poets House, 10 River Terrace, at Warren Street, Battery Park City, Lower Manhattan, (212) 431-7920, poetshouse.org; free.

GINGERBREAD HOUSE DISPLAY, featuring houses designed by bakeries around the city, on view through the end of January in Le Parker Meridien Hotel atrium. Visitors can vote for their favorites by donating $1 to City Harvest; they will also be part of a raffle for a hotel stay. Le Parker Meridien Hotel, 119 West 56th Street, Manhattan, (212) 245-5000, parkermeridien.com.

‘A MONUMENT MORE DURABLE THAN BRASS’ Through Feb. 6, a display of letters, manuscripts, artworks and other items drawn from the Hyde Collection of Johnsoniana, featuring the life and career of the writer Samuel Johnson. The display commemorates the 300th anniversary of his birth. Mondays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Grolier Club, 47 East 60th Street, at Park Avenue, (212) 838-6690, grolierclub.org; free.

MURRAY HILL MARKET AT P.S. 116 An indoor and outdoor market, sponsored by the PTA. Sundays, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.; P.S. 116, East 32nd Street, at Second Avenue, Manhattan; free.

STRAD FOR LUNCH SERIES Every Monday through Feb. 22, at 12:30 p.m., a classical music performance by featured violinists and cellists. For additional weekly performances and details: wmpconcerthall.com. WMP Concert Hall, 31-33 East 28th Street, (212) 582-7536; $10 suggested donation.

THREE KINGS DAY PARADE, presented by El Museo del Barrio. Wednesday, 11 a.m. to 1 p.m., beginning at 106th Street and Park Avenue, with floats, musicians, huge puppets and live animals. (212) 831-7272, elmuseo.org; free.

TREE CHIPPING AND FREE WOOD-CHIP MULCH Through Jan. 9, Brooklyn residents are invited to drop off their Christmas trees for mulching, daily from 8 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. and on Jan. 9, from 10 a.m. to 2 p.m., when master composters from the Brooklyn Botanic Garden will be on hand to discuss the benefits of using wood-chip mulch; free wood chips will be available in exchange for those who bring their trees. Sponsored by the Green-Wood Cemetery and Brooklyn Botanic Garden; Green-Wood Cemetery, Fifth Avenue, at 25th Street, Greenwood Heights, Brooklyn, (718) 768-7300; free.

UNDER THE RADAR 2010 This festival of new theater from around the world continues at various Manhattan locations, including the Public Theater, 425 Lafayette Street, at Astor Place, in the East Village. Schedule and ticket prices: publictheater.org or (212) 967-7555.

‘THE WREATH: INTERPRETATIONS’ Through Wednesday, an exhibition of more than two dozen wreaths made of found and recyclable items, like wine corks, cans, bicycle wheels and garbage ties. Sponsored by the New York City Department of Parks and Recreation. Daily, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Arsenal Gallery, Central Park, 830 Fifth Avenue, at 64th Street, third floor, nycgovparks.org; free.

Spoken Word

SECRETS OF DAN BROWN’S ‘LOST SYMBOL’ Thursday at 7 p.m., the author Lev Grossman leads a discussion with four writers who have studied the mystical, historical and scientific elements of Mr. Brown’s fiction. 92Y Tribeca, 200 Hudson Street, (212) 601-1000, 92y.org/92yTribeca; $12.

Walking Tours

ADVENTURE ON A SHOESTRING Saturday at 2 p.m., “Little Ukraine,” with stops in the East Village, home to a sizable Ukrainian-American population. Meeting at the northwest corner of 10th Street and Second Avenue. (212) 265-2663; $10.

BIG ONION WALKING TOURS Three tours in Manhattan. Friday at 1 p.m., “Historic Times Square” holiday tour, meeting at the southeast corner of 42nd Street and Avenue of the Americas. Saturday at 1 p.m., a tour of the multi-ethnic Lower East Side, through Chinatown, Little Italy and the Five Points district; meeting at the southeast corner of Broadway and Chambers Street. Sunday at 1 p.m., Greenwich Village artists, writers and radicals; meeting at the northeast corner of Seventh Avenue South and Christopher Street. (212) 439-1090, bigonion.com; Each, $15, $12 for students and 63+.

BROOKLYN BREWERY TOUR Saturday, starting at 1 p.m. and then hourly until 5 p.m., a tour of Brooklyn’s popular brewery with tips on the trade. Children must be accompanied by an adult; 79 North 11th Street, Williamsburg, (718) 486-7422, brooklynbrewery.com; free.

FLATIRON DISTRICT Every Sunday at 11 a.m., a free 90-minute tour meeting at the southwest corner of Madison Square Park, at 23rd Street and Broadway, in front of the William Seward statue. Sponsored by the Flatiron/23rd Street Partnership. flatironbid.org.

GOSSIP GIRL TOUR Fridays through Sundays at noon, tours of sites associated with the TV show “Gossip Girl,” including the hotel that is home to the Bass and Van der Woodsen families as well as the Upper East Side mansion that was the site of the Bass/Van der Woodsen wedding. The tour ends with a stop at Humphrey’s favorite bakery. Reservations are required. Details provided at purchase: (212) 209-3370 or screentours.com. $40.

NYC DISCOVERY TOURS Saturday and Sunday at 2 p.m., “Victorian Christmas: Origins of Christmas Traditions” covers the neighborhoods of Gramercy Park, the Flatiron district and Chelsea; at 5:15 p.m., “Historic Greenwich Village Taverns at Christmas.” Reservations and meeting places: (212) 465-3331. $18, drinks are not included.

A TOUR GROWS IN BROOKLYN Daily walking tours of Park Slope and nearby Prospect Park neighborhoods. Meeting place, start times and reservations: (212) 209-3370 or brooklynwalkingtour.com; $35; $15 for children; free for those 2 and under. Lunch is included.

UNION SQUARE: CROSSROADS OF NEW YORK Saturday at 2 p.m., a tour focusing on the political history of the area, meeting by the statue of Lincoln near the 16th Street transverse, Union Square, Manhattan. Sponsored by the Union Square Partnership; (212) 517-1826, unionsquarenyc.org; free. A. E. VELEZ
