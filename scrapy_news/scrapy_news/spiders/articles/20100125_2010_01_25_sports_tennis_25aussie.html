Venus Williams Finds Rhythm and Reaches Quarterfinals

Cameron Spencer/Getty Images

Sixth-seeded Venus Williams defeated Francesca Schiavone, 3-6, 6-2, 6-1, to advance to the quarterfinals of the Australian Open.

  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print

By JOE DRAPE
Published: January 25, 2010

MELBOURNE, Australia — Venus Williams spotted Francesca Schiavone a set on Monday before settling into a rhythm and advancing to the quarterfinals of the Australian Open, 3-6, 6-2, 6-1.

This Grand Slam tournament always vexed Williams, especially of late. She reached the semifinals in 2001 and her best finish was in 2003, when she lost in the final to her sister Serena. Last year, Williams lost in the second round.

She will next meet Li Na of China, who upset fourth-seeded Caroline Wozniacki of Denmark, 6-4, 6-3.

“Francesca plays so well,” Williams said. “She is so tenacious, has so much speed.”

Williams, the sixth seed here, has won seven Grand Slam singles title, including five at Wimbledon, the most recent one in 2008. The other two were on the hardcourts of the United States Open in 2000 and 2001.

Williams was tentative in Monday’s match, dishing out just 3 winners and committing 14 unforced errors in losing the first set. She and Schiavone had trouble holding serve.

When Schiavone, the 17th seed, broke Williams in the opening game of the second set, the crowd readied for an upset. Instead, Williams dug in, winning six of the next seven games to force a third set.

“Well, hey, this is what I practice for 365 days of the year for,” she said. “These moments, these chances — being down a set, a break.” At least one American woman has reached the Australian Open quarterfinals every year since 1977.

Li, who is seeded 16th, and her unseeded compatriot Zheng Jie are the first two Chinese players to make the final eight of the same Grand Slam tournament.

Wozniacki, who had her thigh taped when she was trailing, 3-1, in the second set, struggled to keep up. Li, who held serve at 5-3, cinched it on the second match point after Wozniacki hit a forehand long.

Zheng, a 2008 Wimbledon semifinalist, defeated Alona Bondarenko, 7-6 (6), 6-4, and will meet Maria Kirilenko in the quarterfinals.

NOTES

Andy Roddick battled for five sets in a fourth-round win Sunday. Roddick held off Fernando González, the 2007 Australian Open finalist, 6-3, 3-6, 4-6, 7-5, 6-2, to reach the quarterfinals for the sixth time in eight years. He will meet No. 14 Marin Cilic of Croatia, who ousted the United States Open champion Juan Martín del Potro in five sets. Third-seeded Novak Djokivic beat Lukasz Kubot of Poland, 6-1, 6-2, 7-5, in the fourth round Monday. He will face Jo-Wilfried Tsonga or Nicolás Almagro.

Roddick bucked the trend on a day of upsets that also resulted in fourth-round exits for two of the top women: No. 2 Dinara Safina, last year’s Australian runner-up, and No. 3 Svetlana Kuznetsova, the reigning French Open champion.

“I got a little lucky, but sometimes it’s better to be lucky than good,” Roddick said.

Safina had to retire because of the recurrence of a back injury when she was serving at 5-4 down and 30-40 against Maria Kirilenko.

Kuznetsova lost, 6-3, 3-6, 6-1, to Nadia Petrova, who went into the match after a 6-0, 6-1 third-round victory over the United States Open champion Kim Clijsters. (AP)
