House Hunting in ... Scotland

A FOUR-BEDROOM DUPLEX IN EDINBURGH

A Duplex in Edinburgh

680,000 POUNDS ($1.1 MILLION)

This 2,273-square-foot apartment, updated about 10 years ago, is on the top two floors of a three-story home built around 1870.

A wrought-iron staircase with a mahogany banister leads up to the apartment’s main level, where the drawing room, dining room and sitting room each have fireplaces. The drawing room has a bay window, a decorative ceiling and mirrors inset into the walls. This floor also has an eat-in kitchen and a half bath.

The bedrooms are upstairs. The master has a bay window with a built-in desk and shelving. The en-suite bath has a sunken tub. Of the three additional bedrooms, one is currently used as an office and has built-in shelving and a Murphy bed. Edinburgh Castle can be seen from the bedrooms at the back of the building. There is a full bath on this level, as well as a washer and dryer.

The house is across the street from a communal garden accessible only to residents who live on this street. The neighborhood, called the West End, is an upscale residential area about a 10-minute walk from the city center. Haymarket Train Station is about two minutes’ walk. There is a pedestrian-and-cycling path along the Water of Leith, Edinburgh’s main river, and the Scottish National Gallery of Modern Art is nearby as well. The airport is about 20 minutes’ drive.

MARKET OVERVIEW

The property market peaked in late 2007. Today prices are down 15 to 20 percent, with some higher-end homes seeing declines of about 10 percent.

“The market has changed significantly over the past two years,” said Max Mills, associate director at Rettie & Co., a real estate agency based in Edinburgh, referring to the housing market crash and the global banking crisis.

Prices in central Edinburgh are typically 275 to 350 pounds ($445 to $565) a square foot, Mr. Mills said. Luxury properties in prime residential areas like the West End and New Town range from 375 to 450 pounds ($600 to $725) a square foot; some reach 650 pounds ($1,050) a square foot. This home costs about 300 pounds ($485) a square foot.

Though Edinburgh remains a buyers’ market, there are signs that it has stabilized over the past few months, said Matthew Munro, a partner at Knight Frank, a real estate agency, who heads up the Edinburgh group. “The level of activity will vary upon what it is and where it is, the quality and location,” he said. Edwardian and Georgian-era homes, primarily in or near the city center, have gone up in price 2 to 3.9 percent, while new construction in Edinburgh’s outskirts is faltering.

WHO BUYS IN EDINBURGH

In recent months, since the value of the pound has fallen, Edinburgh has attracted more foreign buyers, mostly from the United States, Asia, Australia and Dubai, according to Mr. Mills.

BUYING BASICS

There are no restrictions on foreign ownership.

Buyers of homes costing more than 125,000 pounds ($200,000) must pay a stamp duty, which ranges from 1 percent of the purchase price for homes up to 250,000 pounds ($400,000) to 3 percent for homes ranging from 250,000 pounds ($400,000) to 500,000 pounds ($800,000). The tax goes up to 4 percent for all properties priced higher, said Gordon Murray, partner at Murray Snell, an Edinburgh law firm.

There is a land registry fee, which ranges from 30 pounds ($50) for homes priced under 50,000 pounds ($81,000) to 7,500 pounds ($12,000) for homes costing more than 5 million pounds ($8.1 million).

Buyers are advised to hire legal help. Rates typically range from about 1,000 ($1,600) to 3,000 pounds ($4,800), depending on the cost of the home, Mr. Murray said. There is also a 17.5 percent value-added tax assessed on the lawyer’s fee.

Acquiring financing can be a challenge, but it is available, Mr. Munro said. All buyers, foreigners and Scots included, should anticipate a minimum down payment of 25 percent, he said. Rates range from 4 to 5 percent, depending on the type of mortgage.

LANGUAGES AND CURRENCY

English, Gaelic, Scots; Pound sterling ($1.62)

TAXES AND FEES

Council taxes are 1,950 pounds ($3,150) a year. Membership in the gardens, which is optional, is approximately 150 pounds ($240) a year, Mr. Mills said.
