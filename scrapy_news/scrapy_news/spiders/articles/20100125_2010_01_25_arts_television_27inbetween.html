Television Review | 'The Inbetweeners'

Dreams of a Teenage Loser

  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
By NEIL GENZLINGER
Published: January 25, 2010

If you buy the old rumors that Disney animators sometimes slipped pornographic images into their artwork, then you can probably also readily imagine that writers of Disney teenage comedies like “Jonas” and “Wizards of Waverly Place” sit around after hours, knock back a few and concoct raunchy versions of their standard scripts.

If that were true, “The Inbetweeners” is what they might come up with: filthy, hilarious and much truer to actual teenage life than anything they produce on company time. But Disney moonlighters can’t take credit for this brash show; it comes from Britain via BBC America, the work of Damon Beesley and Iain Morris, who have a few “Flight of the Conchords” writing credits and a genuine feel for the awkward beast that is teenage life.

The series, which has its premiere on Monday with two episodes before settling into its regular slot on Wednesday, focuses on a 16-year-old named Will (Simon Bird) who is trying to assimilate into a new school but is immediately ostracized. A lazier show would have turned Will into a garden-variety geek, but not this one. Yes, he wears glasses and has a tendency to bungle things, but he’s also savvy and droll, especially in the voice-over commentary he adds to propel each episode along.

Will is mocked by almost everyone at the school, but he does make three friends: Simon (Joe Thomas), Jay (James Buckley) and Neil (Blake Harrison). The four have, basically, two things on their minds: the sex they’re not having and the alcohol they can’t legally buy.

Also, they may be losers, but they aren’t prissies: their language is just as foul as that of the school thugs who are harassing them. Besides creating some difficult editing choices if this show is going to be cleaned up at all for American cable, this language may be off-putting for some viewers: lots of gay insults and sexist blather.

Little is off limits in terms of subject matter either; in two of the first three episodes people with disabilities are the focus of pivotal jokes. But it’s a mark of the show’s intelligence that in both cases it is Will who ends up humiliated.

THE INBETWEENERS

BBC America, Monday night at 9, Eastern and Pacific times; 8, Central time.

Created and written by Damon Beesley and Iain Morris; directed by Gordon Anderson; Mr. Beesley and Mr. Morris, executive producers; Christopher Young, producer. Produced for E4 by Bwark Productions.

WITH: Simon Bird (Will), Joe Thomas (Simon), James Buckley (Jay), Blake Harrison (Neil), Emily Head (Carli), Belinda Stewart-Wilson (Will’s Mom), Robin Weaver (Simon’s Mom), Martin Trenaman (Simon’s Dad), Victoria Willing (Jay’s Mom), David Schaal (Jay’s Dad), Alex Macqueen (Neil’s Dad), Martin Ball (Headteacher) and Greg Davies (Mr. Gilbert).
