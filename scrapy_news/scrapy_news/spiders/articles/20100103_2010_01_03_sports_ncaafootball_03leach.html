Trainer Faults Treatment of Player at Texas Tech

  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints
By THAYER EVANS
Published: January 2, 2010

A Texas Tech athletic trainer said he was told by the fired coach Mike Leach to lock wide receiver Adam James in the dark after doctors determined that he had a concussion, according to an affidavit released Saturday by the university.

In a Dec. 21 interview with a university investigative attorney looking into James’s treatment, Steve Pincock, Texas Tech’s head football trainer, said he told James he was sorry about placing him inside an equipment shed on Dec. 17 near the practice field. According to the affidavit, Pincock also said he was not aware that any other player had ever been treated in such a manner.

“I do not agree with this form of treatment for anyone,” Pincock said in the affidavit.

In a previous statement by Pincock that was provided by Leach’s lawyers, he said that James “was never locked in any facility, and was never placed in an electrical closet or tight space, or instructed to do so.”

Leach was fired Wednesday just before a court hearing seeking a restraining order to allow him to coach in Saturday’s Alamo Bowl. He had been suspended by Texas Tech after James and his family filed a complaint about the player’s treatment.

In the affidavit, Pincock said he told Leach where he had put James and that Leach “was fine with it.”

Leach told The New York Times on Thursday that he ordered James to be taken “out of the light” but did not know where he had been taken because he “was busy coaching practice.” He denied mistreating James.

Leach did not reply to a text message sent to him Saturday.

In describing what Leach told him to do, Pincock quoted the coach as using graphic and profane language about locking James in a dark place, according to the affidavit. Pincock also told the investigator that Leach “wanted James to be uncomfortable,” the affidavit said. Pincock did not return a message left on his cellphone or reply to a text message Saturday.

In another affidavit released Saturday by the university, the team physician Michael Phy told the investigator on Dec. 22 that James “may not have been harmed,” but that Phy considered “this practice inappropriate.” In the statement announcing Leach’s firing, Texas Tech said Leach’s actions put James “at risk for additional injury.”

In a previous statement distributed by Leach’s lawyers, Phy said that “no additional risks or harm were imposed on Adam by what he was asked to do.”

In a statement Saturday, the university said it had Pincock and Phy sign affidavits attesting to their original statements to the investigator after Leach’s “conflicting recent media accounts of the treatment of James.”

Leach told The Times that James acted as if he were entitled to special treatment and that he was lazy. He also said claims of mistreatment by James and his family were the result of bitterness over a lack of playing time.

Additionally, Leach said that he thought that James’s father, Craig James, had tried to use his position as an ESPN analyst to get more playing time for his son.

Through an ESPN spokesman, Craig James described Leach’s claims as “absurd.”

Leach said that Tech used Adam James as an excuse to get rid of him because of lingering ill feelings after contentious contract negotiations last year.
