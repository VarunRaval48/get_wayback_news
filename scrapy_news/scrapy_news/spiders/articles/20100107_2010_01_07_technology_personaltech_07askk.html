Viewing Facebook

Via Roku

Q. Can you see other parts of Facebook besides just photos on the Roku video set-top box?

A. According to the Help section of Roku’s site (roku.com/support/faqs), the video player’s Facebook Channel allows you to see only your photo albums, pictures in which you’ve been tagged and the photos your Facebook friends have posted. You currently cannot use other Facebook staples like your News Feed, games or messaging. You also can’t post comments, update your profile or handle your friend requests.

Roku’s digital video players connect to the television and stream movies from online services like Netflix and Amazon right to the big screen. Some players may need a software update to see some of the newer options, like the Facebook Channel, Flickr and Pandora Radio. Roku sent out an automatic update to all its connected players late last year.

For Roku owners who suspect they may be missing an update, select the Settings icon on the Roku home screen. Select “Player Info” and then “Check for Update” to see if any software updates are available for the video player.

When to Choose

A Bigger Battery

Q. I plan on getting a new laptop soon, but when I look at the specifications for some models online, I see I can upgrade to a 9-cell battery from a 6-cell battery. I assume the bigger model means a longer time before recharging, but are there other things to consider before paying more for the upgrade?

A. Cells are like smaller batteries tucked inside a case to make a bigger battery, so more cells generally means a longer time between charges. Your decision on which to buy depends mainly on how you plan to use the laptop. If you travel frequently or find yourself in situations where you don’t have a power outlet nearby, the 9-cell battery can give you a few more hours of computing time compared with a 6-cell battery.

On the other hand, if you plan to use the laptop mostly at a desk or occasionally unplug it for short bouts of Facebook and Twitter, the 6-cell battery could be just fine — and save you money. A 9-cell battery can cost $40 or more than the standard 6-cell battery included with the laptop.

In addition to the extra cost, longer-lasting batteries tend to be bigger. An extended-life battery often sticks out from the back of the laptop and can make the computer bulkier and heavier to haul around.

Many things affect battery life, including how efficiently the laptop itself uses power and what you do on the computer. A battery may claim to offer six hours of power between charges, but doing things like watching a DVD with the screen brightness turned up all the way drains the juice more quickly than just typing memos in Microsoft Word.

While some laptop manufacturers give you the option to upgrade the battery when you configure a new machine on the company’s Web site, you don’t have to buy a bigger battery right then. You can order it later from the manufacturer or a battery retailer if you find you need more power on the go — or want to have a backup battery handy.
