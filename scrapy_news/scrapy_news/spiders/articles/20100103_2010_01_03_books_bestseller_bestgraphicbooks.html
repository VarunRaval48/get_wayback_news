HARDCOVER GRAPHIC BOOKS
This WeekWeeks on List
1THE BOOK OF GENESIS: ILLUSTRATED, by R. Crumb. (W.W. Norton & Co., $24.95.) The legendary artist tackles the first book of the Bible.10
2THE WONDERFUL WIZARD OF OZ, by Eric Shanower and Skottie Young. (Marvel Entertainment, $29.99.) Dorothy travels to the land of OZ, graphic novel style.14
3PRIDE AND PREJUDICE, by Nancy Butler and Hugo Petrus. (Marvel Entertainment, $19.99.) Jane Austen’s classic tale gets the graphic novel treatment.8
4WOLVERINE: OLD MAN LOGAN, by Mark Millar and Steve McNiven. (Marvel Entertainment, $34.99.) In a future world where the villains are triumphant, Wolverine has left heroics behind to care for his family. If only Hawkeye and the Hulk gang would accept that.4
5BATMAN: BATTLE FOR THE COWL, by Tony Daniel. (DC Comics, $19.99.) Following the "death" of Batman, the protectors and plunderers of Gotham City fight to see who will control the mantle of the bat.4
6BATMAN: THE KILLING JOKE, by Alan Moore and Brian Bolland. (DC Comics, $17.99.) This critically acclaimed story from 1988 offers a possible origin for the Joker.41
7JOKER, by Brian Azzarello and Lee Bermejo. (DC Comics, $19.99.) The Joker, newly released from Arkham Asylum, deals with the rivals who have carved into his territory.30
8ASTERIOS POLYP, by David Mazzucchelli. (Pantheon, $29.95.) The title character is an architect who is suffering from a midlife crisis. But will leaving New York City for small-town America help?7
9BATMAN: WHATEVER HAPPENED TO THE CAPED CRUSADER?, by Neil Gaiman and others. (DC Comics, $24.99.) This collection chronicles the last days of Batman, the secret origin of the villainous Poison Ivy and a tale which depicts the Dark Knight and the Joker as actors in a television show.16
10DARK AVENGERS/UNCANNY X-MEN: UTOPIA, by Matt Fraction, Terry Dodson and others. (Marvel Entertainment, $39.99.) Norman Osborn, the former Green Goblin, sets his eyes on the X-Men. He wants to use his Dark Avengers to destroy and rebuild them under his own control.3
PAPERBACK GRAPHIC BOOKS
This WeekWeeks on List
1LOGICOMIX - AN EPIC SEARCH FOR TRUTH, by Apostolos Doxiadis, Christos H. Papadimitriou, Alecos Papadatos and Annie Di Donna. (Bloomsbury USA, $22.95.) The life of the philosopher and logician Bertrand Russell, and his passion for mathematics, is recounted in this graphic novel.12
2THE ZOMBIE SURVIVAL GUIDE: RECORDED ATTACKS, by Max Brooks. (Three Rivers Press, $17.00.) If you want to survive a zombie attack, there may be no better way than to see how past cultures have done it.11
3WATCHMEN, by Alan Moore and Dave Gibbons. (DC Comics, $19.99.) This epic tale from 1986 signaled a new maturity in comic books.43
4V FOR VENDETTA, by Alan Moore and David Lloyd. (DC Comics, $19.99.) In a dystopian future, an anarchist sets out to topple the oppressive and totalitarian government of the United Kingdom.38
5BONE, VOL. 1, by Jeff Smith. (Scholastic, $8.99.) The nine-volume fantasy series begins here: with the three bone cousins (Fone, Phoney and Smiley) embark on their first adventure filled with magic, rat creatures and allies, young and old.3
6BATMAN: ARKHAM ASYLUM, by Grant Morrison and Dave McKean. (DC Comics, $17.99.) In this critically-acclaimed paint graphic novel, Batman finds himself facing off against his major villains at a home for criminally insane.18
7BATMAN: THE LONG HALLOWEEN, by Jeph Loeb and Tim Sale. (DC Comics, $19.99.) This compelling mystery, set early in the Caped Crusader’s career, has mobsters, the sad downfall of Harvey Dent (Two Face) and a new foe: Holiday.17
8BATMAN: THE DARK KNIGHT RETURNS, by Frank Miller. (DC Comics, $14.99.) Another classic from 1986: The caped crusader comes out of retirement to save Gotham City from a depraved street gang that are more monsters than man.19
9FABLES, VOL. 1, by Bill Willingham and Lan Medina. (Vertigo, $9.99.) After being exiled from their homes, Snow White, Big Bad Wolf and other characters from fairy tales form their own community in New York City.20
10THE UMBRELLA ACADEMY: DALLAS, by Gerard Way and Gabriel Ba. (Dark Horse Comics, $17.95.) The strange team is in shambles. What better time for them to go up against a group of maniacal assassins and, thanks to time travel, a plot to assassinate John F. Kennedy.8
MANGA
This WeekWeeks on List
1NARUTO, VOL. 46, by Masashi Kishimoto. (VIZ Media, $9.99.) Naruto’s friends are threatened, the mysteries of Pain deepen and Naruto must fight to protect his village.12
2BLEACH, VOL. 29, by Tite Kubo. (VIZ Media, $9.99.) Ichigo Kurosaki sees dead people and must help usher them safely into the afterlife.3
3VAMPIRE KNIGHT, VOL. 8, by Matsuri Hino. (VIZ Media, $9.99.) Cross Academy has day and evening students, but the latter have a secret: they are vampires.7
4MAXIMUM RIDE, VOL. 2, by James Patterson and NaRae Lee. (Yen Press, $10.99.) Who knew having wings would be such a problem? Max and her gang travel to New York in search of answers about their past.8
5MAXIMUM RIDE, VOL. 1, by James Patterson and NaRae Lee. (Yen Press, $10.99.) Shouldn’t having wings be fun? That’s not the case for 14-year-old Maximum Ride and her friends who are hunted by a organization called "The School."2
6DEATH NOTE: L, CHANGE THE WORLD, by M. (VIZ Media, $17.99.) The detective known as L is scheduled to die in 23 days. That leaves him with 22 days to stop a terrorist group from unleashing a virus upon the world.7
7FULLMETAL ALCHEMIST, VOL. 21, by Hiromu Arakawa. (VIZ Media, $9.99.) Two brothers harmed in a ritual that was half magic/half science seek the legendary Philosopher’s Stone to make things right. But others seek the weapon of alchemy for their own nefarious means.5
8SOUL EATER, VOL. 1, by Atsushi Ohkubo. (Yen Press, $10.99.) Maka, an arms expert, wants to turn the Soul Eater, her living scythe, into the ultimate weapon for Death.8
9YOTSUBA &!, VOL.7, by Kiyohiko Azuma. (Yen Press, $10.99.) Yotsuba, a little girl with a big personality, sees the wonder in everything. In this installment, she learns about cows.1
10D. N. ANGEL, VOL. 13, by Yukiru Sugisaki. (TOKYOPOP, $10.99.) More about the curse of the phantom thief is revealed. And when Risa’s life is threatened, will Daisuke Niwa be able to save her?3

Rankings reflect sales of graphic novels, for the week ending December 19, at many thousands of venues where a wide range of books are sold nationwide.
