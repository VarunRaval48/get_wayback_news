Museums and Sites

AMERICAN MUSEUM OF NATURAL HISTORY Through Aug. 15, “Traveling the Silk Road: Ancient Pathway to the Modern World”; in conjunction with the exhibition, Saturday and Sunday, noon to 5 p.m., “Voices of the Silk Road,” with performances by musicians from Ukraine, Tibet, Uzbekistan, the Krygyz Republic and other regions. Through May 31, “The Butterfly Conservatory: Tropical Butterflies Alive in Winter,” featuring hundreds of live specimens. In the Imax theater, playing every hour on the half-hour, from 10:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m., “Mysteries of the Great Lakes,” exploring their history and scenery. Timed tickets to “The Butterfly Conservatory,” as well as tickets to Imax films, include museum admission: $24; $18 for students and 60+; $14 for ages 2 to 12; $12 for members and $7.50 for their children. Suggested general admission: $16; $12 for students and 60+; $9 for 2 to 12. Supersaver tickets, including museum and Rose Center admission, the Hayden Planetarium space show (“Journey to the Stars”) and all special exhibitions: $32; $24.50 for students and 60+; $20 for 2 to 12 (adult members, $16; 2 to 12, $11.50). Hours: daily, 10 a.m. to 5:45 p.m. Central Park West and 79th Street , (212) 769-5200, amnh.org.20100114

HISTORIC RICHMOND TOWN Saturday at 7:30 and 9 p.m., a performance by the jug band Wahoo Skiffle Crazies; part of the Tavern Concert Series; $15; $12 for members; reservations required: (718) 351-1611, Ext. 281. Open Wednesdays through Sundays, 1 to 5 p.m.; guided tours, Wednesdays through Fridays at 2:30 p.m.; Saturdays and Sundays at 2 and 3:30 p.m. 441 Clarke Avenue, Richmond Town, Staten Island , historicrichmondtown.org; $5; $4 for 65+; $3.50 for students and children 5 to 17; free for members and children under 5.20100114

NEW-YORK HISTORICAL SOCIETY Sunday at 11:30 a.m., “Murder at the Historical,” an interactive hunt through the Society’s galleries and exhibitions in search of clues to solve a crime based on an actual event; $35; $32 for educators and 65+; $30 for college students; $23 for members. On view through March 25: “Lincoln and New York”; “F. D. R.’s Brain Trust and the Beginning of the New Deal,” an exhibition that focuses on five people who played key roles during Roosevelt’s administration: Raymond Moley, Rexford Tugwell and Adolphe Berle, who were part of the “brain trust,” and Frances Perkins and Harry Hopkins, who were Cabinet members; and “John Brown: The Abolitionist and His Legacy,” which commemorates the 150th anniversary of Brown’s raid on a federal arsenal at Harpers Ferry, Va. (Oct. 16, 1859). Hours: Tuesdays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. (until 8 p.m. on Fridays); Sundays, 11 a.m. to 5:45 p.m.; 170 Central Park West, at 77th Street , (212) 873-3400, nyhistory.org; $12; $9 for educators and 65+; $7 for students; pay what you wish from 6 to 8 p.m. on Fridays; free at all times for members and children under 12.20100114

NEW YORK TRANSIT MUSEUM Through Monday, the eighth annual Grand Central Holiday Train Show, with cars representing the Long Island Rail Road and Metro North, as well as the New York City subway system. Friday and Monday, 8 a.m. to 8 p.m.; Saturday and Sunday, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Beginning Tuesday, the Gallery Annex will be closed for renovations and will reopen in March. Gallery Annex, Grand Central Terminal, near the Stationmaster’s Office , (212) 878-0106, mta.info/mta/museum; free.20100114

Gardens

WAVE HILL Saturday at 1 p.m. and Sunday at 2 p.m., Conservatory walks. Saturday and Sunday, 1 to 4 p.m., Family Art Project: “Bits of Cloth and Lots of Freedom,” a workshop in making a fabric piece inspired by the life of the Rev. Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. Saturday and Tuesday, 11 a.m. to 3 p.m., a workshop in spinning and knitting wool; reservations required: (718) 549-3200, Ext. 305. Grounds open Tuesdays through Sundays, 9 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. Independence Avenue and West 249th Street, Riverdale, the Bronx , (718) 549-3200, wavehill.org; grounds admission: $8; $4 for students and 65+; $2 for children 6 and older; free for members and under 6; free all day on Tuesdays through March and on Saturday mornings from 9 a.m. to noon. On-site parking, $8; free off-site parking with free shuttle service to the garden.20100114

Events

BANANAGRAMS IN THE BASEMENT Saturday at 3 p.m., matches of this anagram game, played like Scrabble, but without the board. Open to all ages. Reservations requested. Word, Franklin and Milton Streets, Greenpoint, Brooklyn , (718) 383-0096, wordbrooklyn.com; free.20100114

FILM SCREENING AND DISCUSSION Tuesday at 7 p.m., an excerpt from “AbUSed — The Postville Raid,” a documentary in progress by Luis Argueta about a raid in 2008 by immigration agents at an Iowa meat-packing plant that employed illegal immigrants. It will be followed by a discussion on immigration policy, enforcement and reform, featuring Mr. Argueta as well as lawyers and members of immigration advocacy groups. Sponsored by the New York Immigration Coalition and the Culture Project. Judson Memorial Church, 55 Washington Square South, Greenwich Village , (212) 627-2227, thenyic.org; donation, $10.20100114

ROBERT FROST’S ANNUAL CHRISTMAS CARDS Through Jan. 30, an exhibition of holiday chapbooks featuring poetry by Frost. Open Tuesday through Friday, 11 a.m. to 7 p.m.; Saturday, 11 a.m. to 6 p.m.; Poets House, 10 River Terrace, at Warren Street, Battery Park City, Lower Manhattan , (212) 431-7920, poetshouse.org; free.20100114

HARLEM LIBRARY CINEMA SERIES, a free screening series, continues Tuesday at 6:15 p.m. with “Banished,” a 2006 documentary by Marco Williams about the forced expulsion of black residents from three communities — Pierce City, Mo.; Harrison, Ark.; and Forsyth County, Ga. — after the Civil War. A discussion with Mr. Williams and others will follow. The series is sponsored by the New York Public Library and the National Black Programming Consortium. 115th Street Branch, 203 West 115th Street, Harlem , (212) 666-9393, blackpublicmedia.org/blackstream.20100114

‘HOLLYWOOD IN THE HEIGHTS’ Wednesday at 7 p.m., the screening of a film montage of clips featuring Brooklyn Heights, followed by a discussion of filmmaking in the area with the director Peter Hedges (“What’s Eating Gilbert Grape,” 1993), who will also introduce the screening. Part of a series of events celebrating the 100th anniversary of the Brooklyn Heights Association, which is also sponsoring the events. St. Francis College Auditorium, 180 Remsen Street , Brooklyn Heights, (718) 858-9193, thebha.org; free, but reservations are necessary: rsvp@austeragency.com.20100114

MUSEUM OF JEWISH HERITAGE Sunday at 2 p.m., “The Road That Heals the Splintered Soul,” a performance of music from Jewish culture and the African diaspora, performed by the ensemble the Afro-Semitic Experience. 36 Battery Place, Lower Manhattan , (646) 437-4202, mjhnyc.org; $15; $12 for children, students and 65+; $10 for members.20100114

NEW YORK CITY RECORD & CD SHOW, also featuring other memorabilia, like T-shirts and posters. Saturday, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. Holiday Inn, 440 West 57th Street, Clinton , (973) 209-6067, showsandexpos.com/rsnyc.htm; $6; preview, 8 to 10 a.m., $25.20100114

WILDERNESS SURVIVAL: PART 2 Sunday at 1 p.m., a workshop led by the Urban Park Rangers in collecting water, the second of four focusing on wilderness survival; the first covered shelter, and the last two will feature fire and food; Forest Park Visitors Center, Woodhaven Boulevard and Forest Park Drive, Queens , (718) 846-2731, nyc.gov/parks/rangers; free.20100114

92ND STREET Y Sunday at 11 a.m., “Books and Bagels,” a discussion with the writers Joyce Carol Oates and Elaine Showalter, with food; $34; $10 for those 35 and younger. Thursday at 8 p.m., an evening with Sam Shepard, who will read from his book “Day Out of Days,” and Patti Smith, who will read from her memoir “Just Kids”; $27; $10 for those 35 and younger. 1395 Lexington Avenue , (212) 415-5500, 92y.org.20100114

HISTORY OF STATEN ISLAND, PART 2 Tuesday at 7 p.m., a discussion about environmental issues on Staten Island, with a look at its museums and parks and other outdoor spaces. Part of a series running through Feb. 2. Staten Island Museum, Building H, Snug Harbor Cultural Center, 1000 Richmond Terrace, Livingston , (718) 727-1135; $12; $8 for members.20100114

INTELLIGENCE SQUARED US Tuesday at 6:45 p.m., “California Is the First Failed State,” a debate on California’s financial problems. Those arguing in favor of the proposition are Andreas Kluth, a writer for The Economist; Robert Sargent Shriver III, a lawyer and member of the Santa Monica City Council; and Sharon Waxman of TheWrap.com. Those arguing against it are Gray Davis, California governor from 1999 to 2003; Van Jones, a lawyer and environmental advocate; and Lawrence O’Donnell Jr., a writer and political analyst for MSNBC. The debate is the first in a spring series; a reception takes place at 5:45 p.m. Skirball Center for the Performing Arts, 566 LaGuardia Place, at Washington Square South, Greenwich Village , (212) 303-4322, intelligencesquaredus.org; $35; $45 includes the reception.20100114

NATIONAL JAZZ MUSEUM IN HARLEM Tuesday at 7 p.m., “Rhythm, Rhythm: Jazz and the World of Lar Lubovitch,” a discussion about jazz and dance with the choreographer Lar Lubovitch and others, including Billy Taylor, the pianist and composer. The evening features a screening of excerpts from performances by Mr. Lubovitch’s dance company. Visitors Center, Suite 2C, 104 East 126th Street , (212) 348-8300, jazzmuseuminharlem.org; free.20100114
