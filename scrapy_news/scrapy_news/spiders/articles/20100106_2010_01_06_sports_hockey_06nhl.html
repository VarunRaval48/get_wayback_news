Brodeur Sets Tone as Devils Shut Out Stars

  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints
By THE ASSOCIATED PRESS
Published: January 5, 2010

Martin Brodeur made 28 saves for his third shutout in seven games, and Patrik Elias scored twice as the Devils beat the visiting Dallas Stars, 4-0, on Tuesday night.

Skip to next paragraph

If it happens on ice and it involves hitting and scoring, The Times Slap Shot blog is on it.

Go to the Slapshot Blog

Brodeur extended his league record by posting career shutout No. 106. He has five this season.

“The team is playing well, and it’s a big team effort anytime you have a shutout,” Brodeur said. “Sometimes it’s like a goal scorer, they come in streaks.”

Travis Zajac and Zach Parise added goals for the Devils, who lead the Eastern Conference.

The Devils opened the scoring when Elias tipped Brian Rolston’s point shot past Alex Auld with 1 minute 58 seconds left in the first.

In the second, Dallas had excellent opportunities to tie the score after Elias was whistled for cross-checking at 4:43, but Brodeur denied Loui Eriksson, and Trevor Daley hit the post. The failed power play was costly as Elias, just out of the penalty box, fired a shot over Auld’s right shoulder to give the Devils a 2-0 lead at 6:59.

Zajac then ended a 12-game goal drought with a score on the power play at 9:36. Parise expanded the Devils’ lead to 4-0 at 18:11.

The focus then turned to preserving the shutout. The Stars generated little sustained pressure in the final period, but Brodeur made a sharp-angle stop on Tom Wandell and a pad save on Eriksson. Elias took a tripping penalty with 3:34 left, giving Dallas a final chance to score. But Brodeur stopped Brad Richards and Mike Modano.

BLACKHAWKS 4, WILD 1 Marian Hossa scored two goals for the second straight game and host Chicago (30-13-3) took over the N.H.L. points lead with its fourth straight victory.

CAPITALS 4, CANADIENS 2 Alex Ovechkin’s debut as Washington’s captain was a success even though he was kept off the score sheet. Tomas Fleischmann had a goal and two assists, and Alexander Semin scored twice to lift the host Capitals.

Washington introduced Ovechkin as the team’s 14th captain.

COYOTES 5, OILERS 4 Shane Doan scored his second goal of the game during a power play 1:37 into overtime to lift visiting Phoenix. Edmonton scored two goals 49 seconds apart in the third to force overtime.

BRUINS 4, SENATORS 1 Blake Wheeler had two goals and an assist for Boston and Tim Thomas extended his winning streak against host Ottawa to 11 games.

IN OTHER GAMES Jonas Gustavsson made 29 saves as host Toronto rallied to beat Florida, 3-2 ... Jordan Staal scored twice and Pittsburgh halted a season-worst five-game losing streak, with a 4-2 win over visiting Atlanta. ... David Moss and Olli Jokinen each scored in the second period, and host Calgary won its fifth straight, 3-1 over Nashville.
