With Its Top Scorer Limited, Seton Hall Defeats No. 9 Pitt

  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints
By THE ASSOCIATED PRESS
Published: January 25, 2010

Herb Pope had 19 points and 9 rebounds Sunday as Seton Hall posted its biggest win in Coach Bobby Gonzalez’s turbulent tenure, 64-61 over No. 9 Pittsburgh in Newark. The Pirates’ leading scorer, Jeremy Hazell, was limited to 9 points and 16 minutes because of foul problems.

“For us to win with Jeremy on the bench with 9 points, it shows we are becoming a good team,” Gonzalez said after his first win over Pittsburgh (15-4, 5-2 Big East).

The last time Seton Hall (12-6, 3-4) beat a top-10 team was March 3, 2006, when it beat Pittsburgh, 65-61, while the Panthers were ranked eighth. Gonzalez took over the next season.

Before the game, the president of Seton Hall, Monsignor Robert Sheeran, denied published reports that Athletic Director Joe Quinlan would be fired at the end of the academic year. The Courier News of Bridgewater, N.J., made the first report late Friday night.

WISCONSIN 79, PENN STATE 71 Jordan Taylor scored the last 8 points of regulation to tie the game, then went on to score 10 more in overtime and lead No. 18 Wisconsin (16-4, 6-2 Big Ten) to a victory over visiting Penn State (8-11, 0-7).

FLORIDA ST. 68, GA. TECH 66 Solomon Alabi made two go-ahead free throws with 22.8 seconds left as visiting Florida State (15-4, 3-2 Atlantic Coast Conference) beat No. 19 Georgia Tech (14-5, 3-3).

N. IOWA 67, INDIANA STATE 58 Kerwin Dunham scored a career-high 14 points, and No. 20 Northern Iowa (17-2, 8-1 Missouri Valley) bounced back from a loss to Wichita State to win at Indiana State (11-9, 3-6).

IONA 68, FAIRFIELD 58 Jermel Jenkins scored 14 points and had 7 assists as Iona (15-6, 7-3 Metro Atlantic Athletic Conference) beat visiting Fairfield (15-6, 7-3).

SIENA 65, MANHATTAN 58 Alex Franklin had 27 points and 10 rebounds as visiting Siena (17-4, 10-0 MAAC) handed Manhattan (7-13, 2-8) its fifth straight loss.

STONY BROOK 67, ALBANY 61 Marcus Rouse had 17 points off the bench to help Stony Brook (14-7, 6-2 America East) defeat visiting Albany (6-16, 1-6).

IN OTHER GAMES St. Peter’s (12-8, 7-3 MAAC) extended its winning streak to five games with a 68-55 victory at Canisius (10-11, 5-5). ... Novar Gadson scored on a tip-in with five seconds left to give Rider (11-11, 4-6 MAAC) a 62-61 victory at Niagara (11-11, 4-6).

Women

DUKE 58, MARYLAND 57 Bridgette Mitchell made the go-ahead basket with 1 minute 29 seconds left, and No. 6 Duke (17-3, 5-0) squeezed by host Maryland (14-6, 2-4) to remain the only unbeaten team in A.C.C. play.

TENNESSEE 55, L.S.U. 43 Alyssia Brewer had 14 points and 7 rebounds to lead No. 3 Tennessee (17-2, 5-1 Southeastern Conference) to a victory at No. 18 Louisiana State (14-4, 3-3).

NOTRE DAME 74, W. VIRGINIA 66 Lindsay Schrader scored 16 points, and No. 4 Notre Dame (17-1, 4-1 Big East) rallied in the second half at home to beat No. 16 West Virginia (18-2, 5-1).

MISSISSIPPI 66, GEORGIA 65 Bianca Thomas hit a 3-pointer with five seconds left to rally Mississippi (14-5, 5-1 SEC) to an upset at No. 8 Georgia (18-2, 5-2).
