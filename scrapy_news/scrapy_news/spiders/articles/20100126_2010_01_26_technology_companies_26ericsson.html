With Profit Down 82% for Quarter, Ericsson Plans More Job Cuts

  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints
By KEVIN J. OBRIEN
Published: January 25, 2010

Ericsson, the world’s biggest maker of mobile-phone networking equipment, said on Monday its fourth-quarter profit fell 82 percent last year and that it planned to cut 1,500 jobs this year, mostly in Sweden, after eliminating 5,000 last year.

The company, based in Stockholm, said sales fell 13 percent in the three months through December, to 58.3 billion kronor ($8.1 billion), from 67 billion kronor in the period a year earlier.

Profit slipped to 700 million kronor ($97.2 million), from 4.1 billion kronor a year earlier.

Slumping sales in Central Europe, Africa and the Middle East hurt the company, which employed 82,500 at the end of December.

“Investors had higher expectations, and those were not met today,” said Mats Nystrom, an analyst with SEB Enskilda Bank in Stockholm. “Network equipment sales are clearly in a decline, and I expect that decline to continue for another one or two quarters at the least.”

Ericsson is facing strong competition from the low-cost Chinese competitors Huawei and ZTE.

By the end of June, Ericsson said it expected to trim 15 billion to 16 billion kronor in annual expenses, roughly 8 percent of sales, through job cuts and other expense reductions.

Ericsson’s stock fell about 2.5 percent in Stockholm.

Investors were also surprised, Mr. Nystrom said, by Ericsson’s decision to spend 28 billion to 30 billion kronor this year, about 15 percent of its sales, on research and development.

Gains by Ericsson’s professional services business, which include managing the wireless networks of global operators, partly offset Ericsson’s slumping network equipment sales. Sales in the services division rose 2 percent, to 16.5 billion kronor in the quarter.

The company’s network equipment sales fell 16 percent in the quarter from a year earlier.

Ericsson also continued to struggle with two money-losing ventures — the cellphone maker Sony Ericsson and the chip maker ST-Ericsson. Sony Ericsson lost 167 million euros ($237 million) in the fourth quarter, and 836 million euros for the year, as the number of handsets it shipped declined 40 percent.

This article has been revised to reflect the following correction:

Correction: January 28, 2010
A headline with an article in some copies on Tuesday about quarterly profits at the cellphone equipment maker Ericsson misstated the period during which profit fell 82 percent. As the article noted, that drop was for the fourth quarter, not for the full year.
