Texas Tops Arkansas to Remain Undefeated

  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
  • Reprints
By THE ASSOCIATED PRESS
Published: January 5, 2010

Dexter Pittman had 21 points and 10 rebounds, and No. 2 Texas remained unbeaten with a 96-85 victory over host Arkansas on Tuesday.

The Longhorns (14-0) made 9 of 11 shots during a 20-9 run that followed a 48-48 halftime tie. Arkansas (7-8) cut the margin to 2 in the final minutes but could not pull even.

Rotnei Clarke scored 24 points for the Razorbacks.

PURDUE 79, MINNESOTA 60 Robbie Hummel and E’Twaun Moore each scored 18 points, and No. 4 Purdue beat visiting Minnesota to remain unbeaten.

JaJuan Johnson had 16 points, 10 rebounds and 4 blocks, and Chris Kramer added 15 points to help the Boilermakers (14-0, 2-0 Big Ten) tie their record for best start. The 1993-94 team that featured Glenn Robinson also won its first 14 games.

Paul Carter scored 15 points and Damian Johnson added 12 for Minnesota (11-4, 2-1), which had won seven straight. Center Ralph Sampson III sat out with an ankle injury.

MISSISSIPPI 84, CEN. FLORIDA 56 Terrico White scored 21 points and No. 14 Mississippi beat host Central Florida. Eniel Polynice had 16 points for the Rebels (12-2), while Chris Warren added 14 and Murphy Holloway had 11 points and 10 rebounds.

A. J. Rompza had 16 points for Central Florida (8-6), which was 0 of 11 from 3-point range and 21 of 62 (34 percent) over all.

GEORGIA 73, GEORGIA TECH 66 Trey Thompkins scored 20 points, Ricky McPhee hit two key baskets near the end and host Georgia pulled off its first big win for its new coach, Mark Fox, knocking off No. 20 Georgia Tech.

Georgia, a team that had lost at home to Wofford and was coming off a 28-point blowout at Missouri last weekend, defeated a team with one of the top-rated freshman classes in the country. The loss was likely to turn up the heat on Yellow Jackets Coach Paul Hewitt, who looked at the scoreboard in disbelief as the Bulldogs (8-5) pulled away. Georgia Tech (11-3) was trying to match its victory total from last season.

women

TEXAS 84, TEXAS-ARLINGTON 41 Brittainey Raven had 17 points and 6 rebounds to lead No. 15 Texas. The host Longhorns (11-3) took a 31-point halftime lead and held the Mavericks (5-8) to 24.6 percent shooting.

XAVIER 68, MISSOURI 60 April Phillips scored 17 points and No. 16 Xavier withstood a late run to snap host Missouri’s seven-game winning streak. Amber Harris had 13 points and 12 rebounds for Xavier (10-3). Christine Flores had 18 points for Missouri (10-3).

WEST VIRGINIA 63, PITTSBURGH 59 Liz Repella scored all 16 of her points in the second half and the No. 22 Mountaineers (14-1, 2-0 Big East) won their 12th straight.
