    Adding World Cup Victory to Family’s Stash

    By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSJAN. 25, 2010

    Inside
      Supported by

      Advertisement

      Continue reading the main story Share This Page

      Felix Neureuther of Germany earned his first career World Cup victory Sunday in Kutzbühel, Austria, winning a slalom that Bode Miller skipped to rest a sore ankle.

      Neureuther, 25, finished in a two-run combined time of 1 minute 37.35 seconds on the icy Ganslern course. His victory came 31 years after his father won in Kutzbühel.

      “A very special feeling, the best day of my life,” said Neureuther, who had finished in the top three six times before. “Being the son of a successful sportsman has always added to the pressure on me.”

      Julien Lizeroux of France, last year’s winner of the Kitzbühel slalom, was 0.39 of a second behind, and Giuliano Razzoli in Italy was third, 0.99 off the pace.

      Miller did not race because his right ankle hurt too much after warm-ups. The United States ski team said he was expected to start at a night slalom Tuesday in nearby Schladming. Ted Ligety was the top American, in 23rd, and Nolan Kasper was 27th.

      The final men’s World Cup races before the Vancouver Olympics will be two giant slaloms and a slalom scheduled for Friday through Sunday in Kranjska Gora, Slovenia.

      Neureuther’s father, Christian, won six World Cup slaloms. His mother, Rosi Mittermaier, won two gold medals and one silver at the 1976 Olympics.

      MAKING A RECOVERY Tanja Poutiainen of Finland won a World Cup giant slalom in Cortina, d’Ampezzo, Italy, and Lindsey Vonn retained her overall lead after finishing 19th.

      Poutiainen posted a two-run combined time of 2:26.51 on the Olympia delle Tofane course, showing that she had recovered from a back problem just in time for the Vancouver Games.

      Viktoria Rebensburg of Germany finished a distant 1.05 seconds behind for her first podium result, and Kathrin Hoelzl of Germany, the giant slalom world champion, was third, 1.45 behind.

      Vonn has a 56-point lead in the overall standings over Maria Riesch of Germany, who was eighth.

      CANADIANS WIN IN SKI CROSS The Canadians Christopher Delbosco and Kelsey Serwa won the World Cup ski cross races at Whiteface Mountain in Wilmington, N.Y. Delbosco beat Andreas Matt of Austria and his Canadian teammate David Duncan across the line. Daron Rahlves of the United States was fourth after a three-week layoff. Serwa beat Switzerland’s Fanny Smith and France’s Ophelie David, the Cup leader, in the women’s final.

      LANGE WINS EUROPEAN TITLE Andre Lange of Germany won the season-ending four-man World Cup bobsled race in Igls, Austria, for the European championship title. Lange teamed with Rene Hoppe, Kevin Kuske and Martin Putze to finish in a two-run combined time of 1:42.59.

      The Swiss team of Ivo Rueegg, which led after the opening run, was second, 0.05 behind, followed by the German team led by Andre Florschuetz, 0.08 behind.

      Steven Holcomb and his United States team had already secured the overall World Cup title last week.

      GERMANY WINS TEAM EVENT Germany won the first Nordic combined World Cup team event of the season, in Schonach, Germany, to increase its lead in the Nations Cup. Georg Hettich, Eric Frenzel, Bjoern Kircheisen and Tino Edelmann combined for 469.3 points from the jumping portion for a 10-second lead in the 4x5-kilometer cross-country race, which they won in 42:49.4 seconds.

      With the top Americans taking a break from World Cup competition to prepare for the Olympics, Bryan Fletcher, Taylor Fletcher, Alex Miller and Nick Henderson combined to give the United States a ninth-place finish. The United States is fifth in the Nations Cup standings.

      1-2 FINISH FOR GERMANS Andrea Henkel led a 1-2 German finish in a women’s 10-kilometer World Cup biathlon pursuit race in Anterselva, Italy. She finished in 30:59.8, missing one shot in the final race of the five-day meet. Magdalena Neuner was second in 31.23.1 after four misses.

      Daniel Mesotitsch of Austria won the men’s 12.5-kilometer pursuit in 31:50.4 with one miss. Tim Burke of the United States did not compete and dropped to fifth in the World Cup standings.
