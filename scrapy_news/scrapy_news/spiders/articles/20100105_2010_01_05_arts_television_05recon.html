Television Review | 'Wild Recon'

A Toxic Workplace, With Ill-Tempered Clients

  • Sign In to E-Mail
  • Print
By NEIL GENZLINGER
Published: January 4, 2010

There are more than 700 reasons not to visit Australia, Donald Schultz tells us, in a backhanded sort of way, in the opening episode of “Wild Recon” on Tuesday night on Animal Planet.

“Australia’s huge range of habitat is home to more than 700 venomous animal species, the most in the world,” says Mr. Schultz, a herpetologist and the gonzo host of this odd but engaging program.

Mr. Schultz is in the business of collecting venom and DNA from unusual, generally uncooperative animals. The venom, he tells us, is for researchers who are studying its medicinal potential; the DNA is for the sake of preserving species that are endangered.

“This is not a stunt,” Mr. Schultz says in what is apparently intended as this program’s catchphrase. “This is my job.”

The first episode finds him all over Australia, tackling a kangaroo and an emu, and plucking poisonous sea snakes from the water at night.

We learn assorted factoids that only a few of us are ever likely to use, like, “The best place to grab a kangaroo is by the tail.” We learn that many of Australia’s native species are in jeopardy because in the 1930s the cane toad, which secretes a toxin fatal to predators, was introduced from Hawaii as an ill-advised pest-control method.

What we don’t learn is why Mr. Schultz and those like him on other dangerous-animal shows have such an aversion to taking obvious precautions. If you were trying to grab a venomous lace monitor, a type of lizard, wouldn’t you put on gloves, or at least roll your shirt sleeves down? Someone cleaning a McDonald’s restroom wears more protective gear than this guy. Sure, he’s entertaining. But don’t try this at home.

WILD RECON

Animal Planet, Tuesday night at 9, Eastern and Pacific times; 8, Central time.

Produced for Animal Planet by Gurney Productions. For Gurney Productions: Scott and Deirdre Gurney, executive producers. For Animal Planet: Lisa Lucas, executive producer; Marc Etkind, vice president of development. Donald Schultz, host.
